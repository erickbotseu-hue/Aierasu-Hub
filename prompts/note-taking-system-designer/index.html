Retrieval needs: [EXAM REVIEW / REFERENCE / PROJECT WORK / NONE] - Tool preference: [APPLE NOTES / NOTION / OBSIDIAN / PAPER / GOODNOTES] NOTE-TAKING SYSTEM: 1. **Method Selection:** | Method | Best For | How It Works | - Cornell: lectures and structured learning - Zettelkasten: long-term knowledge building and connections - Mind mapping: visual learners and brainstorming - Outline: structured, hierarchical content - Flow notes: creative and connection-oriented thinking Recommendation based on your inputs: [SPECIFIC METHOD] 2. **Note Template (Cornell Example):** | Section | Content | When | - Title + date + source: at the top - Main notes (right 2/3): during lecture/reading - Cue column (left 1/3): after — key terms and questions - Summary (bottom): within 24 hours — 3-4 sentence summary 3. **During Capture:** | Rule | Why | - Write in YOUR words — if you're copying, you're not processing - Use abbreviations and symbols — speed matters - Mark confusion with "?" — review later - Capture structure: headings, numbered lists, indentation - Record questions, not just answers 4. **Processing (within 24 hours):** | Step | Duration | Action | - Review notes: fill in gaps while memory is fresh (10 min) - Add cue words: write questions that test understanding - Write summary: 3-5 sentences capturing the key ideas - Connect: link to previous notes on related topics - Flag: mark topics that need deeper study 5. **Review Schedule:** | When | Duration | How | - Same day: 10 min review and enhancement - Day 3: cover main notes, test yourself with cue column - Day 7: review summary, add connections - Day 14: active recall from memory, check against notes - Pre-exam: review summaries and cue columns 6. **Organization:** | Level | How | - By subject/course: clear folder separation - By date: chronological within subjects - Tags/keywords: for cross-subject connections - Index: table of contents updated weekly - Archive: past courses searchable but out of active view CONSTRAINTS: - Notes you never review are wasted effort — build review into your system - Handwriting improves retention, typing improves volume — choose based on your priority - The best system is one you ACTUALLY USE — don't design perfection if you'll abandon it - Less is more — capture key ideas, not everything the professor says - Review > capture — 30 minutes reviewing beats 60 minutes of beautiful notes you never look at again
