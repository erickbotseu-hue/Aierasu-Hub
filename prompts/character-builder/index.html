Creatividad

Character Builder

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Creatividad tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as a character psychologist who creates fictional people so real they could walk off the page — because flat characters kill great plots, and great characters can save mediocre ones. Create a deep, complex character for a [GENRE] story. INPUTS: - Genre: [WHAT TYPE OF STORY] - Role: [PROTAGONIST / ANTAGONIST / LOVE INTEREST / MENTOR / SIDEKICK] - Setting: [WORLD AND TIME PERIOD] - Story theme: [WHAT THE STORY EXPLORES] - Inspiration: [ANY REFERENCE CHARACTERS — or "surprise me"] CHARACTER PROFILE: 1. **Surface Identity:** | Field | Detail | - Name: [WITH MEANING/ETYMOLOGY — names are never random] - Age: [AND HOW THEY RELATE TO THEIR AGE — do they act older? Younger?] - Appearance: distinctive features only (not a police report — what do people NOTICE?) - First impression: what strangers think when they meet this person - Occupation: and how they feel about it 2. **Psychology:** | Dimension | Detail | - Want (conscious goal): what they're pursuing - Need (unconscious): what they actually require for wholeness - Lie they believe: the false belief driving their behavior - Ghost/wound: the formative event that created the lie - Fatal flaw: the weakness that will be tested - Greatest strength: the quality that might save them 3. **Personality & Voice:** - 3 defining traits (with examples of each) - How they speak: vocabulary level, sentence patterns, verbal tics - 5 sample dialogue lines showing their voice - What makes them laugh, cry, and rage - How they behave under stress vs. comfort 4. **Relationships:** | Person | Relationship | Dynamic | Tension | - 3 key relationships that reveal different facets of this character - What they're like with people they trust vs. strangers - Who they love, who they fear, who they envy 5. **Micro-Details (what makes them REAL):** - What's in their pockets/bag right now? - What do they do when alone and nobody's watching? - What's their morning routine? - What's the object they'd grab in a fire? - What smell transports them to childhood? 6. **Arc:** | Phase | State | Trigger | - Beginning: who they are when we meet them - Middle: what challenges this identity - End: who they become (or fail to become) - The cost: what they sacrifice for growth CONSTRAINTS: - Characters must have CONTRADICTIONS — nobody is just "brave" or "kind" - Backstory should influence behavior, not just be trivia — if their childhood doesn't affect their choices, it doesn't matter - Show personality through SPECIFIC DETAILS — "she was kind" is boring; "she left unsigned tips at diners" is alive - The flaw and the strength should be the SAME TRAIT in different contexts - They should surprise the reader (and even you) at least once
