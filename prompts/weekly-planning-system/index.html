Weekly Planning System

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Productividad tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as a productivity coach who designs planning systems that actually get used — because the best system is the one you'll consistently follow, not the most elaborate one. Design a weekly planning system for a [ROLE]. INPUTS: - Role: [JOB TITLE AND KEY RESPONSIBILITIES] - Working hours: [SCHEDULE AND FLEXIBILITY] - Key responsibilities: [TOP 5 AREAS OF FOCUS] - Current challenges: [WHAT'S NOT WORKING ABOUT YOUR CURRENT APPROACH] - Tools: [WHAT YOU USE — calendar, task manager, notes] - Energy patterns: [WHEN ARE YOU MOST/LEAST PRODUCTIVE] WEEKLY PLANNING SYSTEM: 1. **Weekly Review (Friday, 30 minutes):** | Step | Question | Action | - Review completed tasks: what got done? What didn't? Why? - Review calendar: what's coming next week? - Process inbox: emails, notes, voice memos → tasks or archive - Celebrate: what went well this week? (specific wins) - Lessons: what would you do differently? - Commitments: any promises you made that need scheduling? 2. **Weekly Planning (Sunday or Monday, 30 minutes):** | Step | Action | - Set 3 "must-win" outcomes for the week (if NOTHING else happens, these must get done) - Review goals: do this week's tasks move you toward monthly/quarterly goals? - Time-block your calendar: | Block Type | When | Duration | Example | - Deep work: [PEAK HOURS] — creative/analytical tasks - Admin: [LOW ENERGY HOURS] — email, messages, routine tasks - Meetings: batch into 2-3 days (protect 2+ meeting-free days) - Buffer: 20% unscheduled for unexpected items - Prepare for meetings: review agendas, do pre-work - Identify potential obstacles and plan around them 3. **Daily Planning (5 minutes, morning):** - Review today's calendar and commitments - Choose your #1 priority: "If I can only do ONE thing today, it's..." - Set 3 daily goals (the "daily trifecta") - Check energy: adjust task order to match how you feel - 2-minute rule: anything that takes <2 minutes, do it NOW 4. **Daily Shutdown (5 minutes, end of day):** - Review: did I complete my daily trifecta? - Capture: write down any loose threads or ideas - Prep: set tomorrow's #1 priority - Disconnect: close all work apps and tabs 5. **Energy Management:** | Time Block | Energy Level | Best Tasks | - Peak (usually 9-12): deep work, creative, strategic - Moderate (usually 1-3): collaborative, meetings, planning - Low (usually 3-5): admin, email, routine tasks Match task TYPE to energy LEVEL, not just time availability. 6. **Weekly Metrics:** | Metric | Target | How to Track | - Deep work hours: [X hours/week] - Tasks completed vs. planned: [%] - Weekly goals achieved: [X of 3] - Meetings attended vs. necessary: [audit monthly] 7. **When Things Go Off-Plan:** | Scenario | Protocol | - Unexpected urgent task → assess: truly urgent? Reschedule something else - Missed a daily goal → roll to tomorrow, don't add extra - Overwhelming week → cut to 1 must-win, defer the rest - Procrastinating → shrink the task to 15 minutes and start CONSTRAINTS: - Planning should take MAX 30 minutes per week — if it takes longer, simplify the system - The system must work in YOUR tools — don't adopt a new app just for this - Protect deep work time like it's a meeting with your CEO — it's non-negotiable - Don't plan 100% of your time — leave 20% buffer for the unexpected - Review and adjust the system monthly — what's working? What's not?
