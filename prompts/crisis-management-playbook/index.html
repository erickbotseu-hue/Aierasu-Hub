Act as a crisis communications and operations expert who has managed crises ranging from data breaches to PR disasters for companies from startups to public companies. Build a crisis management playbook for [COMPANY]. INPUTS: - Company: [NAME AND DESCRIPTION] - Industry: [INDUSTRY] - Team size: [NUMBER] - Public presence: [social following, press coverage, customer base size] - Biggest risk areas: [DATA BREACH / PUBLIC PR / PRODUCT FAILURE / LEADERSHIP / LEGAL / OTHER] CRISIS PLAYBOOK: 1. **Crisis Classification:** | Level | Description | Example | Response Time | Decision Maker | - Level 1: Minor incident (contained, low impact) — 24-hour response - Level 2: Significant issue (moderate impact, media attention possible) — 4-hour response - Level 3: Major crisis (high impact, active media/social attention) — 1-hour response - Level 4: Existential threat (company survival at stake) — immediate response 2. **Crisis Response Team:** | Role | Person | Responsibilities | Contact | - Incident Commander (CEO/founder) - Communications Lead - Legal Advisor - Technical Lead (for product/data issues) - Customer Success Lead - External PR agency contact 3. **Scenario-Specific Playbooks:** **Scenario A — Data Breach:** - Hour 1: containment steps, legal notification requirements - Hour 1-4: scope assessment, affected users identification - Hour 4-24: customer notification, press statement, regulatory compliance - Template: customer notification email, press statement, social post **Scenario B — Public Relations Crisis (social media/press):** - Hour 1: assess severity, decide response or wait - Hour 1-4: draft statement, legal review, approve - Hour 4-24: publish response, monitor reaction, adjust - Template: holding statement, full response, apology framework **Scenario C — Product/Service Outage:** - Minute 0-15: status page update, internal alert - Minute 15-60: root cause analysis, customer communication - Post-resolution: post-mortem, customer updates, prevention plan - Template: status page messages, email updates, SLA credit policy **Scenario D — Leadership/Employee Crisis:** - Immediate: legal consultation, internal investigation - 24 hours: board/advisor notification, media preparation - 48 hours: public statement if necessary - Template: internal communication, external statement 4. **Communication Templates (Ready-to-Use):** - Holding statement: "We're aware of [ISSUE]. We're investigating and will update within [TIMEFRAME]." - Full response framework: Acknowledge → Empathize → Explain → Actions → Commitment - Customer email template (by severity level) - Social media response templates (short) - Press statement template - Internal team communication template 5. **Monitoring System:** - Tools: social media monitoring, Google alerts, review sites, press mentions - Keywords to monitor - Escalation triggers: what signals warrant activating this playbook? - Sentiment tracking during crisis 6. **Post-Crisis Protocol:** - Post-mortem template: what happened, why, response effectiveness, improvements - Stakeholder follow-up plan - Process updates based on learnings - Team debriefing CONSTRAINT: In a crisis, SPEED and HONESTY matter more than perfection. A genuine, imperfect response in 2 hours beats a polished, lawyer-reviewed response in 48 hours. Every hour of silence erodes trust.
