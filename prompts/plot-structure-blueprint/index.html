Plot Structure Blueprint

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Creatividad tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as a story architect who designs narrative structures that keep readers turning pages — because plot isn't just "what happens," it's the engine of emotional experience. Help me structure a plot for my [GENRE] story about [PREMISE]. INPUTS: - Genre: [TYPE OF STORY] - Premise: [ONE-SENTENCE SUMMARY] - Protagonist: [WHO AND WHAT THEY WANT] - Antagonist: [WHO/WHAT OPPOSES THEM] - Theme: [WHAT THE STORY IS REALLY ABOUT] - Structure preference: [3-ACT / SAVE THE CAT / HERO'S JOURNEY / 7-POINT / KISHOTENKETSU] PLOT BLUEPRINT: 1. **Story Spine:** - Once upon a time: [THE WORLD AND THE STATUS QUO] - Every day: [THE ROUTINE THAT'S ABOUT TO SHATTER] - Until one day: [THE INCITING INCIDENT] - Because of that: [RISING COMPLICATIONS — x3] - Until finally: [THE CLIMAX] - And ever since: [THE NEW NORMAL] 2. **Beat-by-Beat Structure:** | Beat | Scene | Emotional State | Page Target | Using chosen structure, map every major beat with: - What happens (external event) - What changes (internal shift) - The question driving the reader to the next scene 3. **Foreshadowing Plan:** | Planted In | Payoff In | What | How Disguised | - 3 foreshadowing moments that feel inevitable only in retrospect - Plant early, pay off late — the best twists are the ones readers should have seen coming 4. **Subplots (2):** | Subplot | Characters | Theme Connection | Timeline | - Each subplot mirrors or contrasts the main theme - Subplot intersects with main plot at key moments - Subplot resolution reinforces or complicates the main resolution 5. **Tension Escalation:** | Act | Stakes | What Could Go Wrong | What DOES Go Wrong | - Each act raises the stakes (personal → professional → existential) - "Yes, but..." and "No, and..." — every solution creates a new problem 6. **Endings (3 options):** | Type | How It Ends | Theme Statement | Emotional Impact | - Satisfying: protagonist achieves goal and grows - Bittersweet: protagonist grows but at great cost - Dark: protagonist fails or succeeds but loses themselves CONSTRAINTS: - Every scene must TURN — the character's situation must change (emotionally, informationally, or physically) - "And then" is boring; "but" and "therefore" are story — scenes must be CAUSALLY connected - The midpoint should CHANGE EVERYTHING — the story should feel different after it - The climax must test the protagonist's flaw — it's the moment they must face their deepest weakness - No deus ex machina — the protagonist must solve the problem using what they've learned throughout the story
