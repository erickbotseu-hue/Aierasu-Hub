Sales Call Script

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Marketing/Redes Sociales tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Act as a sales trainer who has coached teams to close 30%+ of qualified calls. Your approach is consultative: you diagnose before you prescribe, and you never pressure. Write a sales call script for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. INPUTS: - Product/Service: [DESCRIPTION] - Price: $[AMOUNT] (one-time / monthly / annual) - Average call length: [30 / 45 / 60 MINUTES] - Buyer type: [EXECUTIVE / MANAGER / INDIVIDUAL / TEAM] - Decision process: [SINGLE DECISION MAKER / COMMITTEE / NEEDS APPROVAL] - Competitors: [WHO ELSE THEY'RE LIKELY CONSIDERING] - Close rate goal: [TARGET %] CALL STRUCTURE: 1. **Opening (2-3 minutes):** - Build rapport naturally (reference something from their LinkedIn/website) - Set the agenda: "Here's what I'd love to cover in the next [X] minutes..." - Get permission: "Does that sound good?" - TIME CHECK: "We have [X] minutes — is that still good, or do we need to adjust?" 2. **Discovery (10-15 minutes):** Ask in this ORDER (surface → deep → emotional): **Situation Questions:** | Question | Why You're Asking | - "Tell me about your role and what you're responsible for" - "How are you currently handling [PROBLEM AREA]?" - "What does your current setup/solution look like?" **Problem Questions:** - "What's the biggest challenge with [CURRENT APPROACH]?" - "How long has this been an issue?" - "What's the impact on [REVENUE / TIME / TEAM]?" **Implication Questions:** - "If nothing changes in the next 6 months, what happens?" - "How does this affect your team / your goals / your stakeholders?" - "What has this cost you so far — in time, money, or opportunity?" **Need-Payoff Questions:** - "If you could wave a magic wand, what would the ideal solution look like?" - "What would achieving [DESIRED OUTCOME] mean for you personally?" - "How would your team/boss react if this problem was solved?" 3. **Transition (1 minute):** Summarize what you heard: "So if I understand correctly, you're dealing with [PROBLEM], it's costing you [IMPACT], and you need [SOLUTION] by [TIMELINE]. Is that right?" Get them to say YES before presenting. 4. **Presentation (8-10 minutes):** ONLY present what's relevant to THEIR problems: | Their Problem | Your Feature | Their Outcome | - "You mentioned [PROBLEM] — here's how we handle that..." - "Remember when you said [PAIN]? This is specifically designed for that..." - Show, don't tell: demo the most relevant feature (not everything) - Social proof: "We helped [SIMILAR COMPANY] with exactly this — they saw [RESULT]" 5. **Objection Handling (5-7 minutes):** For each objection, use ARC: Acknowledge → Respond → Confirm | Objection | Acknowledge | Respond | Confirm | | "Too expensive" | "I completely understand budget is a concern" | "Let me break down the ROI..." | "Does that change how you see the investment?" | | "Need to think about it" | "Absolutely, this is a big decision" | "What specific concerns can I help address now?" | "What would help you feel confident?" | | "We're happy with current solution" | "Glad it's working for you" | "What made you take this call today?" | "If we could improve on [THAT], would it be worth exploring?" | | "Need my boss's approval" | "That makes sense" | "What does your boss care about most? Let me help you build the case" | "Should we schedule a call with them together?" | | "Bad timing" | "I get it, timing matters" | "When would be better? I'd hate for you to miss [BENEFIT]" | "Can I pencil in [DATE] to revisit?" | 6. **Close (3-5 minutes):** Choose based on readiness: - Assumptive close: "Great — let's get you started. I'll send the agreement today." - Choice close: "Would the [PLAN A] or [PLAN B] work better for you?" - Summary close: "So we've agreed that [VALUE], [VALUE], and [VALUE] — shall we move forward?" - Next step close: "What's the next step on your end to make this happen?" 7. **Post-Call:** - Send recap email within 1 hour: - Summary of their challenges (in their words) - How you solve them - Pricing and next steps - Deadline or urgency element - If no close: follow-up sequence (Day 1, 3, 7, 14, 30) CONSTRAINTS: - Do NOT present before you diagnose — discovery comes FIRST - Do NOT talk more than 40% of the time — listen more than you speak - Do NOT use high-pressure tactics: "if you sign today…" — this damages trust - Do NOT demo every feature — only show what solves THEIR specific problems - If they're not a fit, TELL them. Honesty builds reputation and referrals - Record every call (with permission) for coaching and improvement
