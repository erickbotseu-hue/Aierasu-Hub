Act as a Head of Growth who has launched 10+ products from zero to $1M ARR. You know that great products fail without great distribution. Design a go-to-market strategy for [PRODUCT] in [MARKET]. INPUTS: - Product: [DESCRIBE WHAT YOU'RE LAUNCHING] - Target market: [WHO AND WHERE] - Price: $[PRICE] - Launch date: [TARGET] - Budget: $[TOTAL GTM BUDGET] - Team: [NUMBER AND ROLES AVAILABLE] - Competitive landscape: [LIST KEY COMPETITORS] - Current assets: [email list, social following, partnerships — what you already have] GTM STRATEGY: 1. **Positioning & Messaging:** - Positioning statement: For [TARGET] who [NEED], [PRODUCT] is a [CATEGORY] that [BENEFIT]. Unlike [COMPETITORS], we [DIFFERENTIATOR]. - 3 messaging angles to test (each targeting a different pain point) - One-line pitch for: website hero, ad headline, sales email, social bio 2. **Channel Strategy (Ranked by Priority):** | Channel | CAC Estimate | Time to Results | Scalability | Budget Allocation | For the TOP 3 CHANNELS, provide specific tactics: - **Channel 1:** [NAME] - Exact steps for first 30 days - Content/creative needed - Tools and resources required - Expected results by Week 4 / Week 8 / Week 12 - **Channel 2:** [NAME] - Same detail level - **Channel 3:** [NAME] - Same detail level 3. **Customer Segmentation & Sequencing:** | Segment | Size | Ease to Reach | Willingness to Pay | Priority | - Which segment to target FIRST (beachhead strategy) - How to expand to subsequent segments (bowling pin strategy) - Messaging variations per segment 4. **12-Week Launch Timeline:** **Pre-Launch (Weeks 1-4):** | Week | Activity | Deliverable | Owner | - Build waitlist/interest - Create launch content assets - Set up tracking and analytics **Launch (Weeks 5-8):** | Week | Activity | Deliverable | Owner | - Launch day tactics - First-week amplification - Early customer activation **Post-Launch (Weeks 9-12):** | Week | Activity | Deliverable | Owner | - Optimize based on data - Scale winning channels - Build case studies from early customers 5. **Sales Strategy (if B2B or high-touch):** - Sales motion: self-serve / sales-assisted / enterprise sales - Sales cycle: expected length and stages - Sales tools: CRM, outreach tools, demo tools - First-meeting script outline 6. **KPI Dashboard:** | Metric | Week 4 Target | Week 8 Target | Week 12 Target | - Traffic → Leads → Trials → Customers → Revenue - Conversion rates at each stage with benchmarks 7. **Risk Mitigation:** | Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation | - What if your primary channel doesn't work? - What if CAC is 2x higher than expected? - What if the market response is lukewarm? 8. **Budget Allocation:** | Category | Amount | % of Total | Expected ROI | - Content creation, paid ads, tools, events, partnerships, buffer CONSTRAINT: A GTM strategy is not a wishlist of channels. It's a SEQUENCED plan that recognizes you can't be everywhere at once. What's the ONE channel that matters most in the first 30 days? Start there. Everything else is a distraction until that works.
