Habit Architecture System

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Crecimiento Personal tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as a behavioral scientist who designs habit systems based on actual neuroscience — because willpower fails, motivation fades, but well-designed systems produce results automatically, and the best habits feel effortless because the ENVIRONMENT does the work. Design a complete habit system for achieving [GOAL]. INPUTS: - Goal: [WHAT YOU WANT TO ACHIEVE] - Current habits: [YOUR EXISTING DAILY ROUTINE] - Time available: [MINUTES PER DAY FOR NEW HABITS] - Past attempts: [WHAT YOU'VE TRIED AND WHY IT FAILED] - Environment: [HOME / OFFICE / TRAVEL — describe your space] - Personality: [REBEL / UPHOLDER / QUESTIONER / OBLIGER — or describe] DELIVERABLES: 1. **Identity Design:** | Current Identity | Target Identity | Evidence Needed | - "What kind of person achieves this goal?" - Identity-based framing: "I am someone who..." (not "I want to...") - 5 small behaviors that prove the new identity 2. **Keystone Habit Selection:** | Candidate Habit | Cascade Effect | Difficulty | Selected? | - The ONE habit that triggers positive domino effects - Why this habit specifically (research-backed) - How it connects to the larger goal 3. **Habit Architecture:** | Element | Design | Psychology | - **Cue**: Environmental trigger (visible, automatic) - **Craving**: Connect to identity/desire - **Response**: Make it EASY (2-minute rule) - **Reward**: Immediate satisfaction layer 4. **Habit Stack:** | Existing Habit | + New Habit | Transition Cue | - Link new habits to existing routines - Morning stack, evening stack, work stack - Each new habit ≤ 5 minutes initially 5. **Environment Design:** | Change | Location | Purpose | Cost | - 5 environment modifications that make the habit automatic - Remove friction for desired behavior - Add friction for undesired behavior - Visual cues placed strategically 6. **Progress System:** | Tracking Method | Frequency | Review Cadence | - Simple tracking (not complex enough to become a barrier) - Weekly review protocol - Monthly milestone check 7. **Failure Protocol:** | Scenario | Response | Prevention | - Miss one day: "Never miss twice" rule - Miss a week: scaled-down re-entry (not full restart) - Complete derailment: identity reminder + environment reset - Self-compassion script (guilt destroys habits faster than laziness) 8. **30/60/90 Milestones:** | Day | Target | Minimum Viable | Celebration | - 30 days: habit established (conscious but consistent) - 60 days: habit automatic (reduced willpower needed) - 90 days: habit identity (this is who I am now) CONSTRAINTS: - Start EMBARRASSINGLY small — a habit you can't skip is better than one you skip "because it's too hard" - Environment > willpower — redesign your space before testing your self-discipline - Never rely on motivation — it's too unreliable; systems work when motivation doesn't - Track ONE habit at a time — adding 5 habits simultaneously guarantees failing all 5 - The 2-minute rule is REAL — if the new habit takes more than 2 minutes initially, make it smaller
