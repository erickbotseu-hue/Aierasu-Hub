Email Welcome Sequence

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Marketing/Redes Sociales tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as an email marketing strategist who designs welcome sequences that achieve 60%+ open rates and generate revenue by Day 7. Write a 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers to [BRAND/PRODUCT]. CONTEXT: - Lead magnet / signup incentive: [WHAT THEY SIGNED UP FOR] - Product/service to eventually sell: [PRODUCT] at $[PRICE] - Brand voice: [DESCRIBE TONE] - Target subscriber: [WHO ARE THEY, WHAT DO THEY WANT] SEQUENCE: **Email 1 — Welcome (Day 0, send immediately)** - Subject line: 3 options - Deliver the lead magnet (link + instructions) - Set expectations: what they'll get, how often - One sentence about who you are (credibility, not autobiography) - Quick win: one micro-tip they can use RIGHT NOW - CTA: "Reply and tell me: what's your biggest struggle with [TOPIC]?" **Email 2 — Connection (Day 1)** - Subject line: 3 options - Your origin story — WHY you care about this topic - Make it vulnerable and specific (not generic hero's journey) - Bridge: how your experience connects to THEIR situation - CTA: read your most popular blog post / resource **Email 3 — Massive Value (Day 3)** - Subject line: 3 options - Your single best piece of advice on [TOPIC] - Make it so good they'd feel guilty NOT paying for it - Framework, template, or step-by-step they can implement today - CTA: share this email with someone who needs it **Email 4 — Social Proof (Day 5)** - Subject line: 3 options - Case study or testimonial (specific results with numbers) - "Before → After" storytelling structure - Subtle seed: mention your product as part of the transformation - CTA: read the full case study **Email 5 — Soft Pitch (Day 7)** - Subject line: 3 options - Bridge from the value you've given → the problem your product solves - Introduce [PRODUCT] naturally (not a hard sell) - 3 bullet points: what it includes, who it's for, key outcome - Risk reversal: money-back guarantee or free trial - CTA: "Learn more" or "Get started" (link to sales page) - P.S. line with urgency or bonus offer FOR EACH EMAIL: subject line (3 options), preview text, full body (150-250 words), one CTA. CONSTRAINT: No email should be longer than 250 words. Respect inbox fatigue. Every sentence must earn its place.
