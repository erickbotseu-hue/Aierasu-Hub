Act as an event content strategist who designs webinars that maintain 80%+ attendance through the end and convert 15%+ of attendees into customers. Write a complete webinar script for "[WEBINAR TITLE]." INPUTS: - Topic: [TOPIC] - Duration: [45 / 60 / 90] minutes - Format: [solo presentation / interview / panel / demo] - Goal: [educate / sell / launch / build authority] - Product to promote (if any): [PRODUCT] at $[PRICE] - Audience: [WHO WILL ATTEND] WEBINAR FLOW: **Pre-Webinar (5 min before start):** - Countdown slide content - Welcome chat messages to warm up attendees - Quick poll to engage early joiners **Opening (0:00-5:00):** - Hook: Bold promise of what attendees will learn (specific outcome) - Housekeeping: how long, Q&A process, recording availability - Credibility: 2-sentence background (results-focused, not autobiography) - Agenda preview: "Here's what we'll cover..." (create anticipation for each section) **Main Content (5:00-35:00):** 3-4 teaching segments, each following this pattern: - Segment headline - Key insight with supporting story, data, or demo - Interactive moment: poll, chat question, or live example - Transition to next segment **The Pivot (35:00-40:00):** — Only if selling - Bridge from teaching to product: "Now, everything I just showed you can be done manually... or you can [PRODUCT]" - Frame as a tool that accelerates what they just learned - NOT a hard sell — a logical next step **Q&A (40:00-50:00):** - Pre-seed 3 questions that address common objections - Handle live questions with succinct, value-driven answers **Closing & CTA (50:00-55:00):** - Recap top 3 takeaways - Special offer (if applicable): limited-time pricing, bonus for attendees - Exact CTA: "Go to [URL] right now and [ACTION]" - Urgency: "This offer expires at midnight tonight / in 48 hours" ALSO INCLUDE: - Slide content suggestions (title + bullet points per slide) - Chat engagement prompts (5 moments to ask the audience something) - Registration page copy: headline + 3 bullet points + urgency element CONSTRAINT: No slide should have more than 6 words of text. Webinar slides support the speaker — they don't replace them.
