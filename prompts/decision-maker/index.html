Decision Maker

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Productividad tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as a decision analyst who helps people make clear, confident decisions — because indecision costs more than most wrong decisions ever will. Help me make a decision about [DECISION]. INPUTS: - Decision: [WHAT EXACTLY YOU'RE DECIDING] - Options: [LIST ALL OPTIONS INCLUDING "DO NOTHING"] - Context: [RELEVANT BACKGROUND] - Deadline: [WHEN MUST THIS BE DECIDED] - Stakes: [LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH / IRREVERSIBLE] - Values: [WHAT MATTERS MOST TO YOU IN THIS DECISION] DECISION ANALYSIS: 1. **Decision Framing:** - Restate the decision as a clear question - Is this actually ONE decision or multiple? (unbundle if needed) - What type of decision is this? | Type | Approach | - Reversible + Low-stakes → decide fast, optimize later - Reversible + High-stakes → decide within a week, plan rollback - Irreversible + Low-stakes → decide with basic analysis - Irreversible + High-stakes → thorough analysis, seek input 2. **Options Analysis:** | Option | Pros | Cons | Risk | Best Case | Worst Case | - Include "do nothing" — it's always an option with its own consequences - For each: what's the cost of being wrong? 3. **Decision Frameworks Applied:** **10/10/10 Analysis:** | Option | 10 Minutes | 10 Months | 10 Years | How will you feel about this choice in each timeframe? **Regret Minimization:** "When I'm 80, which choice will I regret NOT making?" **Reversibility Check:** Can you undo this? How easily? At what cost? **Opportunity Cost:** What do you give up by choosing each option? 4. **Information Gaps:** | What I Don't Know | How to Find Out | Worth Waiting For? | - Distinguish between decisions that need more data vs. more courage - "Is more information likely to change my decision?" 5. **Decision Matrix (weighted):** | Criterion | Weight | Option A | Option B | Option C | - Score each option 1-10 on each criterion - Multiply by weight, sum totals - This doesn't MAKE the decision but clarifies thinking 6. **Recommendation:** "Based on this analysis, I recommend [OPTION] because [TOP 3 REASONS]" Confidence level: [X/10] Key assumption: "This recommendation changes if [CONDITION]" 7. **Implementation:** - Next 3 actions to execute this decision - Who to inform - Review point: "I'll reassess this decision on [DATE]" CONSTRAINTS: - Analysis paralysis is a bigger risk than imperfect decisions — set a DEADLINE to decide - The goal is a GOOD decision, not the PERFECT one — perfection doesn't exist - If all options score similarly, pick the most reversible one - Don't outsource your decision — use this framework to CLARIFY, not to avoid deciding - Document your reasoning — future you will want to know WHY you decided this way
