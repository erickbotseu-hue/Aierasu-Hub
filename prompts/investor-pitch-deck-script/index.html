Act as a pitch coach who has helped founders raise $200M+ in combined funding across Series Seed through Series B. You know what investors actually pay attention to — and what makes them tune out. Write a complete 12-slide pitch deck script for [STARTUP]. INPUTS: - Company: [NAME AND ONE-LINE DESCRIPTION] - Raising: $[AMOUNT] at [SEED / SERIES A / SERIES B] - Valuation target: $[AMOUNT] (if known) - Business model: [HOW YOU MAKE MONEY] - Traction to date: [REVENUE, USERS, GROWTH RATE, KEY METRICS] - Team: [FOUNDERS/KEY TEAM WITH RELEVANT BACKGROUND] - Use of funds: [HOW YOU'LL SPEND THE MONEY] FOR EACH SLIDE, DELIVER: **Slide 1 — Title** - Company name, one-line value prop, logo placeholder - Tagline that communicates what you do AND why it matters - Visual: clean, memorable, professional **Slide 2 — Problem** - Script (60 seconds): tell the problem through a specific person's experience - The "aha moment" statistic that makes the problem feel urgent - Visual: the problem visualized (not just text) **Slide 3 — Solution** - Script (90 seconds): demo-like walkthrough of how your product works - Before/after: life without vs. with your solution - Key differentiator in one sentence **Slide 4 — Market Opportunity** - TAM/SAM/SOM with credible calculations - Why NOW: market timing argument - Visual: market size visualization **Slide 5 — Business Model** - How you make money (specific pricing) - Unit economics: LTV, CAC, LTV:CAC ratio, gross margin - Revenue predictability and expansion potential **Slide 6 — Traction / Proof** - THE SLIDE THAT MATTERS MOST — hard numbers only - Growth chart (the "up and to the right" moment) - Key metrics: revenue, users, growth rate, retention, NPS - Social proof: logos, testimonials, press **Slide 7 — Product** - Screenshot or demo flow - Key features (max 3) tied to customer outcomes - Technology moat (if applicable) **Slide 8 — Competition** - Competitive landscape matrix (NOT a 2x2 where you're in the top-right alone) - Honest differentiation: what you do better AND what competitors do better - Why customers choose you (in their words) **Slide 9 — Go-to-Market** - Customer acquisition channels with CAC per channel - Sales cycle and conversion funnel - Growth strategy: current + next 12 months **Slide 10 — Team** - Founders: relevant experience (why YOU are the ones to build this) - Key hires (or planned hires) - Advisors (if impressive) - Founder-market fit narrative **Slide 11 — Financials** - Revenue projections: 12 months historical + 24 months projected - Key assumptions clearly stated - Path to profitability or next fundraise **Slide 12 — The Ask** - Raising $[AMOUNT] - Use of funds (3-4 categories with percentages) - Milestones this round will achieve - Why NOW is the right time to invest - Contact information FOR EACH SLIDE ALSO INCLUDE: - Presentation script: exact words to say (90-120 seconds per slide) - Visual direction: what should be on the slide (not paragraph text) - Anticipated investor question and prepared answer - Common mistake to avoid on this slide TOTAL PITCH: 15-18 minutes CONSTRAINT: Do NOT put sentences on slides — put EVIDENCE on slides and SAY the sentences. Investors read faster than you talk; if your slides are text-heavy, they'll read ahead and stop listening.
