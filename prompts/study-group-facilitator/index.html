Act as an educational facilitator who designs effective group study processes — because study groups can be the most powerful learning tool or the biggest waste of time, depending entirely on structure. Design a study group framework for [SUBJECT/COURSE]. INPUTS: - Subject: [WHAT YOU'RE STUDYING] - Group size: [NUMBER OF MEMBERS] - Meeting frequency: [HOW OFTEN AND HOW LONG] - Format: [IN-PERSON / VIRTUAL / HYBRID] - Members' levels: [SIMILAR / MIXED / VARIED] - Goal: [EXAM PREP / DEEP UNDERSTANDING / PROJECT / HOMEWORK] STUDY GROUP FRAMEWORK: 1. **Group Norms:** | Norm | Why | - Everyone prepares before the meeting (no freeloading) - Start and end on time - Take turns leading/facilitating - All questions are welcome (no judgment) - Focus during sessions (phones away) - Absent members catch up on their own 2. **Session Structure:** | Phase | Duration | Activity | Who Leads | - Warm-up (5 min): what's everyone stuck on? - Review (15 min): quiz each other on key concepts - Deep dive (20 min): tackle the hardest concepts together - Practice (15 min): work problems collaboratively - Wrap-up (5 min): assign prep for next meeting 3. **Rotating Roles:** | Role | Responsibility | Rotates When | - Facilitator: keeps the group on track and on time - Explainer: teaches one concept to the group - Questioner: prepares challenging questions - Scribe: takes notes and shares after 4. **Collaboration Techniques:** | Technique | How | When | - Teach-back: each person teaches one topic (best for review) - Socratic questioning: ask probing questions, don't give answers - Problem-solving relay: each person does one step of a problem - Debate: argue opposing interpretations of material - Whiteboard sessions: visualize concepts together 5. **Accountability System:** | Mechanism | How | - Prep requirement: share notes/questions before the meeting - Attendance policy: miss 2 in a row → honest conversation - Progress tracking: group goal + individual goals - Peer evaluation: monthly, anonymous feedback CONSTRAINTS: - Groups of 3-5 work best — more than 6 people becomes a lecture, not study - PREPARATION is mandatory — unprepared members waste everyone's time - Teach to learn — the member who EXPLAINS a concept learns it more deeply than everyone else - Social time is fine, but AFTER the session — keep study time focused - If the group isn't working, FIX IT or LEAVE — a bad study group is worse than studying alone
