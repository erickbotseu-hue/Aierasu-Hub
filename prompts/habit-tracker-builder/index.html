Act as a behavioral scientist who designs habit systems based on proven research — because habits are the compound interest of self-improvement. Design a habit tracking system for [3-5 HABITS]. INPUTS: - Habits to build: [LIST SPECIFIC HABITS] - Current consistency: [HOW OFTEN YOU CURRENTLY DO EACH] - Motivation: [WHY EACH HABIT MATTERS] - Obstacles: [WHAT'S STOPPED YOU BEFORE] - Environment: [WHERE AND WHEN YOU COULD DO EACH] - Tracking preference: [APP / SPREADSHEET / JOURNAL / CALENDAR] HABIT SYSTEM: 1. **Habit Design (for each):** | Element | Design | - Cue: what triggers the habit? (time, location, preceding action) - Craving: what desire does it fulfill? - Response: the habit itself (make it TINY to start) - Reward: immediate satisfaction after completing Implementation intention: "I will [HABIT] at [TIME] in [LOCATION] after [CUE]" 2. **Habit Scaling:** | Phase | Duration | Habit Size | Goal | - Week 1-2: Tiny version (2 minutes) → build consistency - Week 3-4: Small version (5-10 minutes) → build capacity - Week 5-8: Target version (full habit) → build permanence - Month 3+: Maintain and never miss twice in a row 3. **Environment Design:** | Habit | Make Easier | Make Harder (bad habits) | - Reduce friction for good habits (lay out gym clothes, prep meals) - Increase friction for bad habits (delete apps, move junk food) - Visual cues: place reminders where you'll see them 4. **Tracking System:** | Date | Habit 1 | Habit 2 | Habit 3 | Notes | - ✅ Done | ❌ Missed | ⚡ Modified version - Track the STREAK — visual chains motivate continuation - Weekly consistency rate: target 80%+ (not 100%) 5. **Accountability:** | Method | How | - Habit partner: share daily check-ins - Public commitment: tell people your intention - Consequence: "If I miss 3 days, I [CONSEQUENCE]" - Reward: "If I hit 30 days, I [REWARD]" 6. **Recovery Protocol:** | Scenario | Response | - Missed 1 day → Never miss twice. Do the tiny version tomorrow - Missed 3+ days → Restart at tiny version, no guilt - Losing motivation → Review your WHY, adjust the habit size - Total failure → Reassess: is this the right habit? Right time? Right approach? CONSTRAINTS: - Start with MAX 3 habits at once — willpower is finite - The 2-minute rule: every new habit starts as a 2-minute version - Never miss TWICE in a row — once is an accident, twice is the start of a new (bad) habit - Track for at least 66 days — research shows that's the average for habit formation - Identity over outcome: "I am someone who [EXERCISES]" is more powerful than "I want to lose weight"
