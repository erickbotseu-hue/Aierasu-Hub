Act as a cognitive performance coach who designs deep work systems based on neuroscience — because focus is a skill that can be trained, not a personality trait you either have or don't. Design a focus/deep work system for me. INPUTS: - Role: [WHAT KIND OF WORK REQUIRES YOUR FOCUS] - Current focus ability: [MINUTES BEFORE DISTRACTION] - Main distractions: [PHONE / EMAIL / COWORKERS / SOCIAL MEDIA / INTERNAL THOUGHTS] - Environment: [HOME OFFICE / OPEN OFFICE / COFFEE SHOP / VARIABLE] - Work type: [CREATIVE / ANALYTICAL / WRITING / CODING / COMMUNICATION-HEAVY] - Schedule constraints: [MEETINGS, FIXED COMMITMENTS] FOCUS SYSTEM: 1. **Environment Setup Checklist:** | Element | Setup | Why | - Physical: desk clear, water bottle, no visual clutter - Digital: close all unnecessary tabs, notifications OFF, phone in another room - Audio: [SILENCE / WHITE NOISE / INSTRUMENTAL MUSIC / NOISE-CANCELING] - Signal: "Do not disturb" light/sign, Slack status, calendar block - Temperature: slightly cool (research shows 70°F / 21°C is optimal) 2. **Pre-Focus Ritual (5 minutes):** | Step | Action | Time | - Clear physical desk (1 min) - Write down the ONE thing you'll work on (1 min) - Close all apps except what you need (1 min) - 10 deep breaths or 1-minute meditation (1 min) - Set timer and begin (1 min) The ritual is a CUE that tells your brain: "It's time to focus now" 3. **Focus Technique (personalized):** Based on current focus ability: | Current | Technique | Session Length | Break | Build To | - <15 min: Micro-sprints (10-15 min focus, 5 min break) → build to 25 min - 15-25 min: Pomodoro (25 min focus, 5 min break, 4 cycles) → build to 50 min - 25-50 min: Extended Pomodoro (50 min focus, 10 min break) → build to 90 min - 50+ min: Flow blocks (90 min focus, 20 min break) → maintain 4. **Distraction Management Protocol:** | Distraction | Prevention | In-the-Moment Response | - Phone: in another room, not on silent (OFF or in a drawer) - Email: check at scheduled times only (3x per day max) - Slack/Teams: status "Focusing until [TIME]", notifications off - Internal thoughts: keep a "capture pad" — write the thought, return to work - Coworkers: headphones on = do not disturb, schedule "office hours" for questions - Urge to check social media: notice the urge, don't act, it passes in 10 minutes 5. **Recovery Protocol:** | After Focus Session | Activity | Duration | - Stand up and move (even 2 minutes) - Look at something far away (20-20-20 rule: every 20 min, 20 sec, 20 feet) - Hydrate - Brief social interaction (if desired) - Do NOT check email/phone during breaks — it resets your focus 6. **Progressive Training:** | Week | Focus Duration | Sessions/Day | Total Deep Work | - Week 1-2: [CURRENT] min × 2-3 sessions = [X] hours - Week 3-4: +10 min per session - Week 5-6: +10 min per session - Goal: 3-4 hours of deep work daily (4 hours is near the human max) 7. **Focus Metrics:** | Metric | How to Track | Target | - Deep work hours per day - Longest unbroken focus session - Focus quality (1-10 self-rating) - Output per focus session - Trend: improving, stable, declining? CONSTRAINTS: - 4 hours of deep work is considered ELITE — don't aim for 8 hours, it's not humanly sustainable - Focus is a MUSCLE — if you can't focus for 25 minutes now, that's normal. Train it. - Your ENVIRONMENT matters more than your willpower — design the environment, don't rely on discipline - Breaks are REQUIRED, not optional — skipping breaks reduces total output - The hardest part is STARTING — the pre-focus ritual solves this
