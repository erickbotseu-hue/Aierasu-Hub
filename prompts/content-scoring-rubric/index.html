Act as a content quality director who reviews thousands of pieces annually. Your rubric has increased average content quality by 40% across teams. Create a comprehensive content scoring rubric for [CONTENT TYPE: blog posts / social media / emails / videos / all]. INTENDED USERS: [writers / editors / freelancers / marketing team] RUBRIC DESIGN: 1. **Scoring Categories (weighted):** | Category | Weight | Score (1-5) | Criteria | Include at least these categories: - Strategic alignment (does it serve a business goal?) - Audience relevance (does it solve a real problem?) - Originality (does it add something new?) - Structure and readability - Writing quality (grammar, style, voice) - SEO optimization (if applicable) - Call to action effectiveness - Visual/formatting quality - Emotional impact / engagement potential 2. **Scoring Definitions:** For EACH category, define what 1, 3, and 5 look like: - 1 = Needs complete rewrite (specific examples of what earns a 1) - 3 = Acceptable, meets minimum standards (specific examples) - 5 = Exceptional, sets the standard (specific examples) 3. **Quality Tiers:** - 90-100%: Publish immediately, showcase - 75-89%: Minor revisions, approve within 1 round - 60-74%: Major revisions needed, return to writer - Below 60%: Reject and reassign 4. **Self-Review Checklist:** 15-item checklist writers complete before submitting (yes/no): Each item must be specific and actionable, not vague 5. **Editor Feedback Template:** Structured format for giving feedback that's clear, constructive, and efficient: - What works well (2-3 specific points) - What needs work (prioritized issues) - Suggested fixes (not just problems — solutions) CONSTRAINT: The rubric must be usable in under 10 minutes per piece. If it takes longer, it won't be used. Prioritize the criteria that matter most for YOUR content type.
