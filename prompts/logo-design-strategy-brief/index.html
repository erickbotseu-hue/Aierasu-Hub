Logo Design Strategy Brief

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Diseño/UX tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as a creative director who writes logo briefs that produce great work the first time — because a bad brief produces a hundred mediocre concepts and a good brief produces three brilliant ones. Create a comprehensive logo design brief for [BRAND]. INPUTS: - Brand: [NAME AND WHAT YOU DO] - Brand personality: [3-5 ADJECTIVES] - Target audience: [WHO NEEDS TO TRUST/LOVE THIS LOGO] - Usage: [WHERE THE LOGO WILL APPEAR — digital, print, merchandise, signage] - Preferences: [LOGOS I ADMIRE AND WHY — share 3-5 examples] - Anti-preferences: [LOGOS I DISLIKE AND WHY] - Budget/process: [HIRING A DESIGNER / DIY / AI TOOLS] DELIVERABLES: 1. **Logo Type Exploration:** | Type | Example Brands | Fit For Your Brand | Recommendation | - Wordmark (Google, Coca-Cola) - Lettermark (IBM, HBO) - Symbol (Apple, Twitter) - Combination (Adidas, Spotify) - Emblem (Starbucks, Harley-Davidson) 2. **Style Direction:** | Direction | Mood | Color Family | Typography Style | Reference | - Direction A: [describe mood, style, feeling] - Direction B: [alternative approach] - Direction C: [wildcard/unexpected] 3. **Technical Requirements:** | Requirement | Specification | - Scalability: favicon (16px) to billboard - Color versions: full color, single color, reverse, grayscale - File formats needed - Background adaptability - Animation potential (for digital use) 4. **Evaluation Criteria:** | Criterion | Weight | Test | - Memorability: can you draw it from memory after 5 seconds? - Scalability: readable at 16px? - Versatility: works on dark AND light backgrounds? - Relevance: communicates the brand personality? - Uniqueness: distinguishable from competitors? CONSTRAINTS: - A logo is not the brand — it's a SYMBOL of the brand; stop expecting it to communicate everything - Simple > clever — the best logos are instantly recognizable; complexity fails at small sizes - Trends expire — design for 10 years, not 10 months; avoid trendy treatments - Test in context — a logo on a white background means nothing; test on business cards, websites, and products - Invest in the BRIEF, not just the design — a great brief produces great options; a vague brief produces endless revisions
