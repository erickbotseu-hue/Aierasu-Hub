Act as a data storyteller who transforms numbers into narratives that drive action — because the gap between "we have data" and "we made a decision" is always a well-told story. Write a data-driven report on [TOPIC] for [AUDIENCE]. INPUTS: - Topic: [WHAT THE REPORT COVERS] - Audience: [WHO READS THIS — C-suite, board, team, investors, clients] - Key findings: [YOUR TOP 3-5 DISCOVERIES FROM THE DATA] - Data sources: [WHERE THE NUMBERS CAME FROM] - Time period: [WHAT TIME RANGE THE DATA COVERS] - Tone: [TECHNICAL / EXECUTIVE / GENERAL AUDIENCE / INVESTOR-FRIENDLY] - Goal: [INFORM / PERSUADE / ALARM / CELEBRATE] REPORT STRUCTURE: 1. **Executive Summary (1 paragraph):** The "if you read nothing else" paragraph — contains the single most important insight, the key number, and the recommended action. A busy executive should be able to make a decision from this paragraph alone. 2. **Key Findings (3-5):** | # | Finding | Supporting Data | Confidence Level | Impact | For each finding: - One-sentence headline (the insight, not the data point) - Supporting data with visual (described: "a line chart showing...") - Context: how this compares to benchmarks, prior periods, or expectations - "So what?": why this finding matters for the business 3. **Trend Analysis:** | Metric | Direction | Rate of Change | Projection | Driver | - What's changing and at what rate - Root cause analysis: WHY it's changing - Whether the trend is accelerating or decelerating - 3-month and 6-month projections (with confidence ranges) 4. **Data Visualizations (described):** | # | Chart Type | What It Shows | Why This Chart | Key Takeaway | - 4-6 charts with specifications - Each chart tells ONE story - Title format: "[Insight], [Time Period]" not "[Variable] by [Variable]" 5. **Implications & Recommendations (3-5):** | # | Recommendation | Supporting Evidence | Expected Impact | Investment Required | Priority | - Actionable recommendations ranked by impact/effort - Each tied directly to a finding - Include expected outcome if recommendation is followed 6. **Methodology & Limitations:** | Element | Detail | - Data sources and collection methods - Sample sizes and time periods - Known limitations and biases - What additional data would strengthen conclusions - Confidence level of each major claim 7. **Appendix:** - Detailed data tables - Methodology deep-dive - Glossary of terms CONSTRAINTS: - LEAD with insight, not method — "Revenue grew 23%" first, "we analyzed Q3 data using..." last - Every chart needs a HEADLINE that tells the story — "Revenue Growing Fastest in Southeast" not "Revenue by Region" - Numbers need CONTEXT — $1M revenue is great for a startup, terrible for a Fortune 500 - Recommendations must be SPECIFIC and ACTIONABLE — "improve marketing" is useless; "increase paid search budget by 15% in Southeast region targeting ages 25-34" helps - Acknowledge UNCERTAINTY — overconfident reports lose trust; honest caveats build credibility
