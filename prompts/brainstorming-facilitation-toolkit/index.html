Act as a creative facilitator who runs brainstorming sessions that produce usable ideas — not just sticky notes that get thrown away. Build a brainstorming facilitation toolkit with 5 techniques for different types of problems. INPUTS: - Problem type: [STRATEGIC / CREATIVE / TECHNICAL / PROCESS / PRODUCT] - Group size: [2-5 / 5-10 / 10+] - Time available: [30 / 60 / 90 MINUTES] - Virtual or in-person: [FORMAT] - Team dynamics: [OPEN AND VOCAL / QUIET AND RESERVED / MIXED] BRAINSTORMING TOOLKIT: 1. **Technique 1 — Brainwriting (for quiet teams):** | Step | Action | Time | - Each person writes 3 ideas silently (5 min) - Pass papers to the right, build on others' ideas (5 min × rounds) - Group discussion and clustering (10 min) - Dot voting on best ideas (5 min) Best for: introverted teams, avoiding groupthink 2. **Technique 2 — Reverse Brainstorming (for stuck problems):** | Step | Action | Time | - "How could we make this problem WORSE?" (10 min) - List all ways to fail - Flip each failure into a solution (10 min) - Prioritize solutions (10 min) Best for: problems where direct solutions aren't working 3. **Technique 3 — SCAMPER (for product/process improvement):** | Letter | Prompt | Ideas | - Substitute: what can you replace? - Combine: what can you merge? - Adapt: what can you borrow from elsewhere? - Modify: what can you change? - Put to other use: how else can this be used? - Eliminate: what can you remove? - Reverse: what can you flip? Best for: improving existing products/processes 4. **Technique 4 — Crazy 8s (for rapid visual ideas):** | Step | Action | Time | - Fold paper into 8 sections - 1 minute per section: sketch one idea - Share and discuss all sketches - Vote on most promising Best for: design and UI problems, visual thinkers 5. **Technique 5 — Six Thinking Hats (for complex decisions):** | Hat Color | Perspective | Questions | - White: facts and data — "What do we know?" - Red: emotions and intuition — "How do we feel about this?" - Black: risks and problems — "What could go wrong?" - Yellow: benefits and optimism — "What's the best case?" - Green: creativity — "What are unconventional options?" - Blue: process — "What's our conclusion?" Best for: complex decisions with multiple stakeholders 6. **Session Structure (any technique):** | Phase | Time | Activity | - Setup (5 min): define the problem clearly, ground rules - Diverge (40-60%): generate as many ideas as possible - Converge (30-40%): group, evaluate, prioritize - Commit (10%): select ideas, assign next steps 7. **Ground Rules:** - Quantity over quality (during diverge phase) - No criticism during idea generation - Build on others' ideas ("yes, and...") - Encourage wild ideas (they often lead to practical ones) - One conversation at a time - Stay on topic CONSTRAINTS: - Define the problem CLEARLY before brainstorming — vague problems produce vague ideas - Diverge THEN converge — mixing the two kills creativity - Every session must end with NEXT STEPS — ideas without action are entertainment - Include quiet ideation time — the loudest person shouldn't dominate - Follow up within 48 hours — momentum dies fast
