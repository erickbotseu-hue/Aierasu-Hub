Innovation Workshop Design

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for SaaS/Business tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as an innovation facilitator who has run 100+ workshops for companies from startups to Fortune 500s, consistently producing actionable ideas that get funded and built. Design a high-impact innovation workshop for [TEAM/COMPANY]. INPUTS: - Workshop goal: [generate new product ideas / solve a specific problem / reimagine a process / explore a new market] - Specific challenge: [THE PROBLEM OR OPPORTUNITY TO ADDRESS] - Participants: [NUMBER] people, [ROLES/DEPARTMENTS] - Time available: [2 HOURS / HALF DAY / FULL DAY] - Physical or virtual: [IN-PERSON / REMOTE / HYBRID] - Innovation maturity: [never done this / occasional / regular practice] WORKSHOP DESIGN: **Pre-Workshop (1 week before):** - Pre-read materials: what participants should review (keep under 10 minutes) - Provocation questions: 3 questions to start thinking before the session - Data packet: key facts, customer insights, market data to inform ideation - Logistics: materials needed, room setup, or virtual tools **Workshop Flow:** **Phase 1 — Warm-Up & Divergent Thinking (20% of time):** - Ice-breaker exercise: [specific activity tied to theme, not generic] - "Worst Idea" exercise: intentionally generate terrible ideas to break creative blocks - Brain-writing: individual idea generation (6 ideas per person, 3 minutes each) - No criticism allowed in this phase — quantity over quality **Phase 2 — Problem Deep-Dive (20% of time):** - Customer empathy exercise: walk through a day in the customer's life - "5 Whys" analysis: dig to the root cause of the problem - Reframe the challenge: "How might we...?" statement (3 variations) - Constraint removal: "What would we do if we had unlimited budget? Zero tech constraints? 10x the team?" **Phase 3 — Structured Ideation (25% of time):** - Technique 1: SCAMPER (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other use, Eliminate, Reverse) - Technique 2: Analogy thinking ("How does [DIFFERENT INDUSTRY] solve a similar problem?") - Technique 3: "The Future Press Release" — write a press release for the innovation 2 years from now - Cross-pollination: teams share ideas and build on each other's concepts **Phase 4 — Evaluation & Selection (20% of time):** - Idea clustering: group similar ideas into themes - Feasibility/impact matrix: plot ideas on 2×2 (high impact + easy = quick wins) - Dot voting: each participant gets 3 votes - Top 5 ideas selected for prototyping **Phase 5 — Rapid Prototyping & Pitching (15% of time):** - 15-minute rapid prototype: sketch, storyboard, or one-page concept - 3-minute pitch per team: problem, solution, first step, resource ask - Feedback protocol: structured feedback (what excites me, what concerns me, what I'd add) **Post-Workshop:** - Decision framework: how leadership will evaluate and fund top ideas - 30-day sprint plan for the top 2-3 ideas - Innovation metrics: how to measure if workshop outcomes created value - Follow-up cadence: check-ins at 2 weeks, 4 weeks, 8 weeks **FACILITATOR SCRIPT EXCERPTS:** - Opening: exact words to set the tone (psychological safety, permission to be bold) - Energy dip recovery: backup activities when energy drops at the 60% mark - Closing: how to end with momentum and clear next steps CONSTRAINT: Innovation workshops fail when they produce ideas that nobody acts on. The workshop must end with CLEAR ownership, timeline, and resources for the top ideas. Otherwise, it's just a fun meeting.
