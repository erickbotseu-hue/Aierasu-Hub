Energy Audit

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Productividad tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as a performance psychologist who helps people align their work with their energy — because managing energy is more important than managing time. Create an energy audit mapping activities that energize vs. drain you. INPUTS: - Activities last week: [LIST ALL WORK AND PERSONAL ACTIVITIES] - Energy level: [HOW YOU FELT THROUGHOUT THE WEEK] - Role: [YOUR JOB] - Observations: [PATTERNS YOU'VE NOTICED ABOUT YOUR ENERGY] ENERGY AUDIT: 1. **Activity Energy Map:** | Activity | Hours/Week | Energy Impact (-5 to +5) | Essential? | Category | Categories: - 🟢 Energizing (+3 to +5): you feel MORE alive after doing this - 🟡 Neutral (-2 to +2): neither drains nor energizes significantly - 🔴 Draining (-3 to -5): you feel depleted after doing this 2. **Energy Patterns:** | Time of Day | Energy Level (1-10) | Best Activities | - Map your natural energy curve across the day - Identify: peak hours, maintenance hours, recovery needed 3. **Energy Audit Summary:** | Category | Activities | Total Hours | % of Week | - Energizing activities: [HOURS AND %] - Neutral activities: [HOURS AND %] - Draining activities: [HOURS AND %] Target: energizing ≥ 40%, draining ≤ 20% 4. **Optimization Plan:** | Draining Activity | Can Eliminate? | Can Delegate? | Can Redesign? | Can Batch? | For each draining activity: - Eliminate: stop doing it entirely - Delegate: assign to someone who finds it energizing - Redesign: change HOW you do it to reduce the drain - Batch: group draining tasks to contain the damage - Schedule: do draining tasks during high-energy periods 5. **Energy Recovery Strategies:** | Drain Type | Recovery Activity | Time Needed | - Social drain → solitary activity (walk, reading) - Mental drain → physical activity (exercise, movement) - Creative drain → structured task (admin, cleanup) - Emotional drain → connection (trusted friend, journaling) 6. **Redesigned Week:** | Day | High Energy Block | Medium | Low | Recovery | - Schedule energizing activities in strategic positions (start of day, after draining tasks) - Sandwich draining tasks between energizing ones - Protect recovery time — it's not optional CONSTRAINTS: - Energy management ≠ avoiding hard work — some draining activities are essential and valuable - Track for at least 2 weeks to see real patterns, not just one bad week - Your energy profile is UNIQUE — don't copy someone else's schedule - Recovery is PRODUCTIVE — it enables future high performance - The goal is SUSTAINABILITY, not maximizing output until burnout
