Act as a pricing strategist who has optimized pricing for 50+ SaaS and digital products, consistently achieving 20-40% revenue lifts through strategic pricing changes. Optimize the pricing strategy for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. INPUTS: - Product: [WHAT YOU SELL] - Current pricing: [CURRENT MODEL, TIERS, AND PRICES] - Target customer: [WHO PAYS] - Competitors and their pricing: [LIST 2-3] - Current metrics: MRR $[X], customers [X], ARPU $[X], churn [X%] - Price sensitivity: [HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW — or unknown] PRICING ANALYSIS: 1. **Current Pricing Audit:** - Value metric: are you charging for the right thing? - Price point: are you too cheap, too expensive, or just right? - Packaging: does your tier structure align with customer segments? - Competitive position: where do you sit relative to alternatives? - Revenue leakage: where are you leaving money on the table? 2. **Value Metric Analysis:** | Potential Value Metric | Alignment with Value | Ease of Measurement | Predictability | - Recommend the optimal value metric and justify - Show how switching value metrics would change revenue 3. **Pricing Model Optimization:** | Current | Recommended | Reasoning | Expected Revenue Impact | - Redesigned tier structure: 3-4 tiers with clear differentiation - Feature allocation: what goes in each tier (use the "2/3 rule" — free tier gets 2/3 of features) - Naming strategy: tier names that communicate value, not just size 4. **Price Point Optimization:** - Competitive price positioning: below / at / above market — and WHY - Price anchoring strategy: how to make the target tier look like the best deal - Psychological pricing: charm pricing ($29 vs. $30), decoy pricing, bundling - Annual vs. monthly: recommended discount (typically 15-20%) 5. **Price Increase Playbook:** If recommending a price increase: - Grandfather strategy: keep existing customers at old price? For how long? - Communication plan: email template announcing the increase - Timing: when to announce vs. when to implement - Churn prediction: expected churn from price increase and how to mitigate - Net revenue impact projection 6. **Expansion Revenue Strategy:** - Usage-based upsells: seats, storage, API calls, features - Cross-sell opportunities: complementary products - Enterprise tier: custom pricing for large accounts - Target: [X]% of revenue from expansion by Month [Y] 7. **Pricing Experimentation Plan:** | Test | Hypothesis | Method | Duration | Success Metric | - A/B test design for the most impactful pricing change - Segmented pricing tests (by geography, company size, or channel) CONSTRAINT: Never optimize pricing based on costs alone. Price based on VALUE delivered. A product that saves customers $10,000/year can charge $2,000/year regardless of whether it costs $5 or $500 to deliver.
