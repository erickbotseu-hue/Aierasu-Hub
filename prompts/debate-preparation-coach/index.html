Debate Preparation Coach

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Educación tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as a debate coach who prepares people to argue persuasively on any topic — because the ability to construct and defend arguments is a life skill, not just an academic exercise. Prepare me to debate [TOPIC] on the [PRO/CON] side. INPUTS: - Topic/motion: [THE DEBATE PROPOSITION] - Your side: [FOR / AGAINST] - Format: [FORMAL DEBATE / DISCUSSION / PANEL / MEETING] - Audience: [WHO WILL HEAR THIS] - Time limits: [MINUTES PER ARGUMENT] - Opponents' likely strategy: [WHAT YOU THINK THEY'LL ARGUE] DEBATE PREP: 1. **Your Core Argument (in 60 seconds):** "We believe [POSITION] because [3 REASONS], and the evidence shows [KEY DATA]" 2. **3 Main Arguments:** | # | Argument | Evidence | Impact | Emotional Appeal | For each: - Claim: what you're arguing - Evidence: data, research, examples - Impact: why it matters (who benefits, who gets hurt) - Emotional hook: story or scenario that makes it REAL 3. **Counter-Arguments (what they'll say):** | Their Argument | Your Rebuttal | Evidence | - Anticipate their 5 strongest points - Prepare rebuttals for each - "They'll say [X], but actually [Y], because [EVIDENCE]" 4. **Logical Fallacies to AVOID (and SPOT):** | Fallacy | Example | How to Counter | - Straw man: misrepresenting their argument - Ad hominem: attacking the person, not the argument - Appeal to authority: "experts say" without citing who - Slippery slope: exaggerating consequences - False dilemma: presenting only two options 5. **Rhetorical Techniques:** | Technique | Example | When to Use | - Rule of three: group points in threes - Anaphora: repetition for emphasis - Rhetorical question: make the audience think - Storytelling: humanize the abstract - Pause: silence after a powerful point 6. **Opening and Closing Statements:** - Opening: hook + position + roadmap (60 sec) - Closing: summarize + strongest point + memorable call to action (60 sec) CONSTRAINTS: - Attack ARGUMENTS, not people — ad hominem is a sign of a weak case - Acknowledge valid opposing points — it makes you MORE credible, not less - Evidence must be SPECIFIC — "studies show" is weak; "a 2024 Harvard study found X" is strong - Your strongest argument should go LAST — end on your strongest note - Practice OUT LOUD — debate is a performance, not an essay
