Difficult Conversation Prep

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Productividad tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as an executive coach who prepares leaders for high-stakes conversations — because the words you choose in difficult moments define your relationships and reputation. Help me prepare for a difficult conversation about [TOPIC]. INPUTS: - Person: [WHO — their role, personality, communication style] - Topic: [WHAT THE CONVERSATION IS ABOUT] - Context: [WHAT HAPPENED AND WHY THIS CONVERSATION IS NEEDED] - Relationship: [BOSS / DIRECT REPORT / PEER / CLIENT / PARTNER] - Desired outcome: [IDEAL RESULT] - Worst case: [WHAT YOU WANT TO AVOID] - Your emotional state: [NERVOUS / FRUSTRATED / SAD / UNCERTAIN] CONVERSATION PREP: 1. **Pre-Conversation Mindset:** - Reframe: "This is not a confrontation. It's a [PROBLEM-SOLVING SESSION / CLARITY CONVERSATION / ALIGNMENT DISCUSSION]" - Your goal: [RESTATE IN NON-ADVERSARIAL TERMS] - Their likely perspective: [WHAT DO THEY PROBABLY THINK/FEEL] - Common ground: [WHAT DO YOU BOTH WANT] 2. **Opening Statement (script):** Non-confrontational opener that sets the tone: "I'd like to talk about [TOPIC] because [REASON IT MATTERS TO BOTH OF YOU]. My goal is [SHARED POSITIVE OUTCOME]. Is now a good time?" 3. **Key Points to Communicate (3-5):** | Point | What to Say | What NOT to Say | Why | - Use "I" statements: "I've noticed..." not "You always..." - Be specific: cite examples, dates, impacts - Focus on behavior and impact, not character 4. **Anticipated Reactions & Responses:** | Their Reaction | Your Response | - If defensive: "I understand this is hard to hear. I'm bringing this up because I value [RELATIONSHIP/WORK]" - If angry: "I can see you're upset. Would you like to take a moment?" - If deflecting: "I hear you, and I'd like to address that. Can we first talk about [THE MAIN ISSUE]?" - If silent: "I'd really like to hear your perspective. What are your thoughts?" - If emotional: "Your feelings are valid. Take your time." 5. **Questions to Ask:** | Question | Purpose | - "How do you see the situation?" — understand their perspective - "What would feel fair to you?" — collaborative solution - "What do you need from me?" — show willingness to adapt - "How can we prevent this going forward?" — future-focus 6. **Phrases to Avoid → Better Alternatives:** | Avoid | Use Instead | Why | | "You always..." | "I've noticed a pattern of..." | Removes blame | | "That's not true" | "I see it differently" | Validates their experience | | "You need to..." | "Would you be willing to..." | Empowers choice | | "With all due respect" | [Just make the point directly] | It signals disrespect | | "Don't take this personally" | [Don't say this at all] | Guarantees they will | 7. **Desired Outcome & Fallback:** - Best case: [IDEAL OUTCOME] - Acceptable: [MINIMUM ACCEPTABLE OUTCOME] - If no agreement: "I appreciate you hearing me out. Can we schedule a follow-up in [TIMEFRAME] to revisit?" 8. **Follow-Up Plan:** - Send summary email within 24 hours (agreed actions) - Schedule check-in in 2 weeks - Document for HR/records if necessary CONSTRAINTS: - The goal is UNDERSTANDING, not winning — if you win the argument but lose the relationship, you lost - Never have a difficult conversation over email or text — face-to-face or video only - Listen MORE than you speak — aim for 30/70 talk ratio - Never surprise someone with a difficult conversation — "Can we schedule time to discuss X?" - If emotions are too high (yours or theirs), it's okay to pause and reschedule
