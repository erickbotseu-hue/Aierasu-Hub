INPUTS: - Context: [COMPANY / UNIVERSITY / COMMUNITY / PROFESSIONAL NETWORK] - Size: [NUMBER OF MENTORS AND MENTEES] - Duration: [MONTHS] - Goals: [CAREER DEVELOPMENT / SKILL BUILDING / DIVERSITY / RETENTION] - Format: [1:1 / GROUP / PEER / REVERSE / HYBRID] MENTORSHIP PROGRAM: 1. **Program Structure:** | Element | Content | - Duration: [X] months, [FREQUENCY] meetings - Format: [1:1 / group / peer circles] - Time commitment: [HOURS PER MONTH] - Meeting length: [MINUTES] - Virtual/in-person: [FORMAT] 2. **Matching Criteria:** | Criterion | Weight | How to Assess | - Goals alignment (most important) - Industry/functional area overlap - Communication style compatibility - Availability match - Diversity considerations - NOT: exact same background (diversity of perspective is valuable) 3. **Meeting Templates (by month):** | Month | Theme | Discussion Topics | Activity | - Month 1: get to know each other, set goals - Month 2-3: skill/knowledge focus - Month 4-5: challenges and problem-solving - Month 6: reflection, next steps, celebration 4. **Goal-Setting Framework:** | Goal | Measurable Outcome | Mentor's Role | Timeline | - Mentee defines 3 goals at program start - Mentor helps refine and create action plan - Monthly check: on track? 5. **Program Management:** | Activity | Frequency | Owner | - Check-in surveys: monthly - Group events: quarterly - Mid-point review: month 3 - Final evaluation: last month - Feedback collection: continuous CONSTRAINTS: - Mentees drive the relationship — mentors guide, not dictate - Match based on GOALS, not seniority alone — a great mentor is someone 2-3 steps ahead, not 20 - Structure prevents abandonment — meetings without agendas get skipped - Both mentors and mentees should benefit — mentors gain leadership skills and fresh perspectives - TRAIN mentors — great professionals aren't automatically great mentors
