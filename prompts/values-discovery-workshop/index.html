Crecimiento Personal

Values Discovery Workshop

💡 Ideal Use Case

High-value utility for Crecimiento Personal tasks.

🚀 Pro Tip

Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as an executive coach specializing in values-based decision-making — because people who know their values make faster decisions, experience less regret, and build lives that feel authentically theirs instead of accidentally someone else's. Guide me through a comprehensive values discovery process. INPUTS: - Life stage: [STUDENT / EARLY CAREER / ESTABLISHED / TRANSITION / RETIREMENT] - Recent decisions I'm proud of: [LIST 2-3] - Recent decisions I regret: [LIST 2-3] - People I admire: [LIST 3 AND WHY] - Current major tension: [WHERE LIFE FEELS MISALIGNED] DELIVERABLES: 1. **Values Exploration (40 values by category):** | Category | Values | Your Reaction | - Achievement: success, mastery, excellence, recognition, ambition - Connection: love, family, belonging, community, loyalty - Freedom: independence, adventure, autonomy, flexibility, spontaneity - Growth: learning, wisdom, creativity, curiosity, self-improvement - Impact: service, justice, generosity, leadership, legacy - Integrity: honesty, authenticity, courage, responsibility, fairness - Wellbeing: health, balance, peace, joy, mindfulness - Security: stability, safety, comfort, predictability, wealth 2. **Elimination Tournament:** | Round | Values Remaining | Decision Logic | - Round 1: 40 → 20 (gut reaction: "yes" or "not essential") - Round 2: 20 → 10 (forced choice pairs: "if you could only keep one...") - Round 3: 10 → 5 (rank by: "which would I sacrifice last?") 3. **Deep Value Definitions:** For each top 5 value: | Value | What It Means to ME | How I Know I'm Living It | How I Know I'm NOT | - Personal definition (not dictionary) - Behavioral indicators (what does this look like in action?) - Violation indicators (what happens when I compromise this?) 4. **Values Conflict Resolution:** | Scenario | Value A vs. Value B | My Choice | What This Reveals | - 3 realistic scenarios where your top values compete - Which value wins and why — revealing hidden hierarchy - How to honor both when possible 5. **Alignment Audit:** | Value | Current Alignment (1-10) | Evidence | One Change to Improve | - How well your current life reflects each value - Where the largest gaps exist - One specific change per value 6. **Personal Values Statement:** - 2-3 sentence manifesto - Decision-making filter: "When in doubt, I choose..." - Annual review reminder CONSTRAINTS: - Values are DISCOVERED, not chosen — help me see what I already value through my behavior, not what I think I should value - No "aspirational" values — if I don't act on it, it's not a value; it's a wish - Values conflict is NORMAL — the goal isn't to eliminate tension but to understand hierarchy - Test against real decisions — my stated values should explain my best decisions and predict where I'll struggle - Keep it practical — a values list that sits in a drawer is decoration, not direction
