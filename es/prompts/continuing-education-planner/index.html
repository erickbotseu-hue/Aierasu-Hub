Act as a professional development strategist who keeps careers current — because in every field, the half-life of knowledge is shrinking. Design a continuing education plan for [PROFESSION]. INPUTS: - Profession: [YOUR FIELD] - Current certifications: [WHAT YOU HOLD] - CE requirements: [REQUIRED HOURS/CREDITS] - Career goals: [WHERE YOU'RE HEADING] - Budget: $[ANNUAL FOR DEVELOPMENT] - Time: [HOURS PER QUARTER AVAILABLE] - Learning preferences: [CONFERENCES / ONLINE / PEER / SELF-STUDY] CE PLAN: 1. **Requirement Tracking:** | Certification | CE Required | Deadline | Earned | Remaining | - Track all requirements in one place - Set reminders 6 months before deadlines - Document everything (proof of completion) 2. **Strategic Learning Plan:** | Quarter | Focus Area | CE Hours | Activities | Cost | - Q1: [TOPIC aligned with career goals] - Q2: [EMERGING TREND in your field] - Q3: [CROSS-FUNCTIONAL skill] - Q4: [RENEWAL requirements + reflection] 3. **Learning Sources:** | Source | Type | CE Value | Cost | Quality | - Conferences: networking + learning (1-2/year) - Online courses: structured learning (quarterly) - Webinars: staying current (monthly) - Peer learning: journal clubs, study groups (ongoing) - Publications: reading key journals (weekly) - Teaching/presenting: deepest learning (as available) 4. **Return on Learning:** | Learning Activity | Skills Gained | Applied How | Impact | - Track what you LEARNED and how you APPLIED it - Not just "I attended X" but "I now do Y differently because of X" 5. **Annual Review:** | Question | Response | - What's changing in my field? - What skills are becoming more important? - What skills are becoming obsolete? - Am I learning strategically or just checking boxes?
