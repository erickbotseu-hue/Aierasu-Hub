Act as a generational fiction specialist who designs family sagas spanning decades — because the most compelling stories are about how love, trauma, and ambition echo through generations. Create a three-generation family saga outline spanning [HISTORICAL PERIOD]. INPUTS: - Time span: [START YEAR — END YEAR] - Location: [WHERE THE FAMILY IS BASED — may change over generations] - Historical events: [REAL EVENTS THAT AFFECT THE FAMILY] - Core theme: [THE THREAD THAT CONNECTS ALL THREE GENERATIONS] - Family culture: [ETHNICITY / RELIGION / SOCIAL CLASS / PROFESSION] - Starting point: [THE FOUNDING EVENT / SECRET / DECISION] SAGA OUTLINE: 1. **Generation 1 (Founders):** | Character | Role | Arc | Legacy | - The matriarch/patriarch: who they are and the world that shaped them - The founding decision: the choice that sets everything in motion - The family secret: what gets buried (and will resurface) - Historical context: how the era constrains and enables them 2. **Generation 2 (Inheritors):** | Character | Role | Arc | Inheritance | - How they carry (or reject) their parents' legacy - The family dynamic: siblings who handle inheritance differently - The secret's effect: how the buried truth shapes their lives without their knowledge - Their own mistake: a new wound added to the family's story 3. **Generation 3 (Reckoning):** | Character | Role | Arc | Resolution | - The generation that uncovers the truth and must decide what to do with it - How they see their grandparents' era (with distance) - The climactic revelation: when the family secret surfaces - Resolution: does the family cycle break or repeat? 4. **Recurring Motifs:** | Motif | Gen 1 | Gen 2 | Gen 3 | - An object passed through generations (with changing meaning) - A phrase or saying that transforms in meaning across eras - A location that appears in each generation's story - A pattern of behavior that repeats (or finally breaks) 5. **Historical Integration:** | Event | How It Affects the Family | Which Character | - Real historical events that serve as turning points - How the same family values help or hinder in different eras - The gap between public history and private family history CONSTRAINTS: - Each generation must stand alone as a compelling story — not just "set up" for the next - Historical events should AFFECT the family personally — not just be background decoration - The family secret should cast SHADOWS, not just be a surprise — its effects should be visible even before the revelation - Theme must be universal — the specific family should illuminate something about ALL families - End with ambiguity — does the cycle break?
