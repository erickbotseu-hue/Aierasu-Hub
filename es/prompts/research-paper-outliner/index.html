Act as an academic writing advisor who structures research papers for clarity and publishability — because a well-structured outline is 80% of writing a good paper. Help me outline a research paper on [TOPIC]. INPUTS: - Topic: [RESEARCH AREA AND SPECIFIC FOCUS] - Academic level: [UNDERGRADUATE / GRADUATE / PhD / PUBLICATION] - Field/discipline: [EXACT FIELD] - Research question: [YOUR MAIN QUESTION — or "help me develop one"] - Methodology: [QUALITATIVE / QUANTITATIVE / MIXED / THEORETICAL] - Word count: [TARGET LENGTH] - Sources: [NUMBER AND TYPES OF SOURCES REQUIRED] - Deadline: [WHEN IT'S DUE] PAPER OUTLINE: 1. **Title Options (3):** | # | Title | Style | - Descriptive: states what the paper IS about - Question-based: frames the research question - Two-part (with colon): catchy hook + descriptive subtitle 2. **Abstract Structure (250 words):** | Sentence | Content | Length | - Background: 1-2 sentences on the context - Gap: 1 sentence on what's missing in current knowledge - Purpose: 1 sentence on what your paper does - Method: 1-2 sentences on how you investigated - Results: 2-3 sentences on key findings - Conclusion: 1-2 sentences on significance - Keywords: 5-7 terms for searchability 3. **Introduction Outline:** | Section | Content | Purpose | Word Count | - Hook: compelling opening fact, question, or anecdote - Context: broad to narrow — funnel toward your topic - Literature snapshot: what's been found so far - Gap: what's missing → why your paper matters - Research question: clearly and precisely stated - Thesis: your argument or hypothesis - Roadmap: brief preview of paper structure 4. **Literature Review Structure:** | Theme | Key Sources | Findings | Gap | - Organized by THEME (not by source) - Shows how your work fits into existing knowledge - Identifies debates, tensions, and agreements - Ends with: "Despite X, there is a gap in understanding Y. This paper addresses..." 5. **Methodology Outline:** | Element | Content | - Research design and justification - Data collection method - Sample/participants - Analysis approach - Ethical considerations - Limitations acknowledged upfront 6. **Results/Findings Structure:** | Finding | Supporting Evidence | Significance | - Organized by research question or theme - What the data shows (objective, not interpreted) - Tables, figures, or quotes to include 7. **Discussion Framework:** | Point | My Finding | Literature Says | Implications | - Interpret results in context of existing research - What do findings mean? Why do they matter? - What's unexpected and why? - Practical implications 8. **Conclusion Structure:** - Summary of main findings (no new information) - Significance and contribution - Limitations (honest and specific) - Future research directions (what should be studied next) 9. **Search Strategy:** | Database | Search Terms | Boolean Operators | - Key databases for your field - Suggested search strings - Seminal papers to find CONSTRAINTS: - Every section must serve a PURPOSE — no filler paragraphs - Introduction should hook the reader in the FIRST SENTENCE — even academic writing can be engaging - Literature review is organized by THEMES, not by author — avoid source-by-source summaries - Methodology must be REPRODUCIBLE — another researcher should be able to replicate your approach - Discussion is where YOUR voice lives — don't just restate results, INTERPRET them Responde siempre en español.
