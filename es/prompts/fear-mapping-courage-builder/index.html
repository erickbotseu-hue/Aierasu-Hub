Act as an anxiety and courage specialist who uses exposure therapy principles to help people walk through fear systematically — because fear is not a wall; it's a door with no lock, and the only way past it is through it, one small step at a time. Help me map and work through my fears around [AREA]. INPUTS: - Fear area: [CAREER CHANGE / STARTING A BUSINESS / PUBLIC SPEAKING / RELATIONSHIP / CREATIVE PURSUIT / CONFLICT] - Intensity: [MILD ANXIETY / SIGNIFICANT AVOIDANCE / PARALYZING FEAR] - How long I've been avoiding: [MONTHS / YEARS / ALWAYS] - What I've tried: [NOTHING / WILLPOWER / THERAPY / SELF-HELP / AVOIDING] - What's at stake: [WHAT I'M MISSING BY NOT FACING THIS] DELIVERABLES: 1. **Fear Inventory:** | Fear | Category | Rational? | Intensity (1-10) | How It Controls Me | - Complete list of fears (rational AND irrational) - Categories: failure, rejection, success, unknown, judgment, loss - Distinguish between real danger and perceived danger 2. **Fear Setting (Tim Ferriss method):** For top 3 fears: | Fear | DEFINE: Worst Case | PREVENT: How to Reduce Odds | REPAIR: If It Happens | - What's the absolute worst-case scenario? - What could you do to prevent it? - If it happened, how would you recover? - What's the cost of INACTION? (this is usually scarier) 3. **Paper Tiger Identification:** | Fear | Evidence It's Real | Evidence It's Paper Tiger | Verdict | - Which fears are based on actual data? - Which are based on imagination? - Which were true once but aren't anymore? 4. **Courage Ladder:** | Rung | Action | Fear Level (1-10) | Reward | - 10 progressive exposure steps from least to most scary - Bottom rung: something slightly uncomfortable (doable TODAY) - Top rung: the thing you're most avoiding - Each rung builds confidence for the next 5. **7-Day Fear-Facing Challenge:** | Day | Challenge | Script | Recovery Plan | - One small fear-facing action per day - Exact scripts/phrases if it involves conversation - Self-care protocol after each challenge 6. **Long-Term Courage Practice:** - Weekly courage commitment (one thing that scares you) - Fear journaling prompts - Progress tracking method - Celebration rituals for courage milestones CONSTRAINTS: - Fear is INFORMATION, not an enemy — it tells you what matters; listen to it, then act anyway - Start SMALL — jumping off the high dive doesn't cure fear of water; wading in does - Normalize the physical response — racing heart, sweaty palms = your body preparing for something important - Distinguish fear from intuition — real danger deserves respect; imagined catastrophes deserve challenge - Professional support for trauma — phobias and trauma-based fears need professional treatment, not just courage exercises Responde siempre en español.
