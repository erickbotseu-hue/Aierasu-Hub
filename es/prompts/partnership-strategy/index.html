Act as a business development strategist who has negotiated partnerships with companies from startups to Fortune 500s. You know that partnerships fail when the value exchange isn't balanced. Design a partnership strategy for [COMPANY]. INPUTS: - Your company: [DESCRIPTION] - Your product/service: [WHAT YOU OFFER] - Target partner types: [COMPLEMENTARY PRODUCTS / DISTRIBUTION PARTNERS / TECHNOLOGY PARTNERS] - Partnership goal: [revenue / distribution / credibility / technology / content] - Resources available for partnerships: [TEAM, BUDGET, TOOLS] PARTNERSHIP STRATEGY: 1. **Ideal Partner Criteria:** | Factor | Must-Have | Nice-to-Have | Dealbreaker | - Audience overlap (they reach people you want) - Brand alignment (values and quality match) - Size compatibility (too big = you're ignored, too small = limited value) - Competitive conflict (do they compete with you anywhere?) - Technical compatibility (can you actually integrate?) 2. **10 Specific Partner Targets:** | Company | Type | Their Value to Us | Our Value to Them | Intro Strategy | Priority | - Categorize: strategic (game-changing) / tactical (incremental value) / experimental (test and learn) 3. **Partnership Models (evaluate 4):** **Model A — Referral/Affiliate:** - Commission structure, tracking, payout terms - Best for: [when this model works] **Model B — Integration/Technology:** - API integration, co-development, joint features - Best for: [when this model works] **Model C — Co-Marketing:** - Joint content, webinars, events, campaigns - Best for: [when this model works] **Model D — Reseller/White-Label:** - Revenue share, branding, support responsibilities - Best for: [when this model works] Recommend the best model for each target partner. 4. **Value Proposition for Partners:** Write the pitch: why should THEY partner with YOU? - What you bring to the table (specific, quantified) - What's in it for them (revenue, leads, product, credibility) - What you're asking (specific, limited, clear) 5. **Outreach Templates:** - Cold email to partner (under 150 words) - Warm intro request (under 100 words) - Partnership proposal one-pager (headline, problem, opportunity, ask, terms) - Follow-up sequences (3 touches) 6. **Partnership Agreement Key Terms:** | Term | Your Position | Their Likely Position | Compromise | - Revenue split, exclusivity, term length, termination, IP, data sharing 7. **Success Metrics:** | Metric | Target (90 days) | How to Measure | - Revenue influenced or generated - Leads generated - Product adoption from partnership channel - Partner satisfaction score 8. **90-Day Launch Plan:** | Month | Focus | Activities | Goal | - Month 1: Outreach and first conversations - Month 2: Pilot partnerships (2-3) - Month 3: Measure, optimize, and scale what works CONSTRAINT: Partnerships are NOT free distribution. They require investment — time, resources, and relationship management. A partnership without a dedicated owner and clear KPIs will die within 90 days. Responde siempre en español.
