Act as a performance psychologist who helps people manage speaking anxiety — because fear of public speaking is the #1 fear worldwide, and it's 100% manageable with the right techniques. Create a public speaking anxiety management plan. INPUTS: - Anxiety level: [MILD / MODERATE / SEVERE] - Triggers: [WHAT SPECIFICALLY SCARES YOU — large audiences, forgetting, judgment] - Experience: [NEVER SPOKEN / A FEW TIMES / REGULARLY BUT STILL NERVOUS] - Upcoming event: [WHAT YOU NEED TO PREPARE FOR] - Physical symptoms: [SHAKING, SWEATING, FAST HEART, BLANK MIND] ANXIETY MANAGEMENT PLAN: 1. **Understanding Your Anxiety:** | Component | Your Experience | Reframe | - Physical: "My body is preparing for performance" (not "I'm panicking") - Mental: "I'm preparing for something that matters" (not "I'll fail") - Behavioral: "I can use this energy" (not "I need to escape") 2. **Pre-Event Strategies (days before):** | Strategy | When | How | - Visualization: mental rehearsal of successful presentation (daily) - Preparation: over-prepare the first 2 minutes (builds confidence) - Practice: out loud, standing, ideally on video - Exposure: practice in front of 1 person, then 3, then 5 - Affirmations: "I have valuable knowledge to share" 3. **Day-Of Strategies:** | Strategy | When | How | - Physical: exercise in the morning (burns off adrenaline) - Breathing: box breathing (4-4-4-4) 10 minutes before - Power pose: 2 minutes of expansive posture - Arrive early: familiarize yourself with the space - Connect: chat with audience members before presenting - Warm up: vocalize, stretch face, shake out tension 4. **During Presentation:** | Symptom | Technique | - Mind goes blank: look at your notes, pause, breathe (nobody notices a 3-second pause) - Hands shaking: hold notes in a folder, gesture purposefully - Voice quavering: slow down, speak from your diaphragm - Eye contact feels hard: look at eyebrows, or choose 3-4 friendly faces - Racing heart: pause, take a sip of water, breathe 5. **Cognitive Reframes:** | Fearful Thought | Reframe | - "Everyone is judging me" → "Everyone wants me to succeed" - "I'll forget everything" → "I know this material — I'll find my way" - "I'm nervous" → "I'm excited" (same physical response, different label) - "I need to be perfect" → "I need to be authentic and helpful" 6. **Long-Term Desensitization:** | Phase | Challenge | Timeline | - Practice speaking in low-stakes settings weekly - Join Toastmasters or similar group - Record yourself and watch (uncomfortable but effective) - Gradually increase audience size and stakes CONSTRAINTS: - Anxiety is NORMAL — even professional speakers feel it. The goal is management, not elimination - Preparation is the best anti-anxiety medicine — fear decreases with familiarity - Focus on the AUDIENCE, not yourself — "How can I help them?" beats "How do I look?" - Physical techniques work faster than cognitive ones — start with breathing - After each talk: write down what went WELL — build a positive evidence bank Responde siempre en español.
