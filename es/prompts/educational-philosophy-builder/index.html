Act as a philosophy of education scholar who helps educators articulate their teaching philosophy — because the best teachers know not just WHAT and HOW they teach, but WHY. Help me develop my educational philosophy statement. INPUTS: - Your role: [TEACHER / PROFESSOR / TRAINER / TUTOR / INSTRUCTIONAL DESIGNER] - Subject: [WHAT YOU TEACH] - Experience: [YEARS AND CONTEXT] - Audience: [WHO YOU TEACH] - Purpose: [TENURE APPLICATION / PROFESSIONAL WEBSITE / PERSONAL CLARITY / JOB APPLICATION] - Length: [PARAGRAPH / 1 PAGE / 2 PAGES] PHILOSOPHY DEVELOPMENT: 1. **Core Beliefs Exploration:** | Question | Your Response | - What is the PURPOSE of education? - What is the ROLE of the teacher? - What is the ROLE of the student? - How do people ACTUALLY learn best? - What does a GREAT learning experience look like? - What do I want students to REMEMBER 10 years from now? 2. **Influences Mapping:** | Influence | What I Learned | How It Shows in My Teaching | - A teacher who changed your life (and what they did) - An educational theory that resonates (and why) - A learning experience that failed (and what it taught you) - Your own learning journey (and what shaped it) 3. **Theory Connection:** | Theory | Core Idea | How It Aligns | - Constructivism: learners build knowledge from experience - Behaviorism: learning through reinforcement - Humanism: learning as personal growth - Social learning: learning through observation and collaboration - Pragmatism: learning through practical application - Critical pedagogy: education as liberation 4. **Evidence from Practice:** | Belief | How I Implement It | Evidence It Works | - For each core belief: specific examples from your teaching - Student outcomes, feedback, or stories that demonstrate your philosophy - How your philosophy has evolved over time 5. **Philosophy Statement Structure:** | Section | Content | Length | - Opening: your core belief about education (1-2 sentences) - Beliefs: what you believe about learning and teaching (2-3 paragraphs) - Practice: how your beliefs show up in your teaching (2-3 paragraphs) - Evidence: outcomes and examples (1-2 paragraphs) - Growth: how your philosophy continues to evolve (1 paragraph) 6. **Peer Review:** | Reviewer | What to Ask | - "Does this sound like me?" - "Does it connect beliefs to specific practices?" - "Is it authentic, or does it sound like a textbook?" - "Would you want to learn from this person?" CONSTRAINTS: - Be AUTHENTIC — your philosophy should sound like YOU, not a textbook - Show HOW your beliefs translate to PRACTICE — abstract beliefs without examples are empty - Include GROWTH — show that you're still learning as a teacher - Be SPECIFIC — "I believe in student-centered learning" is vague; describe what that looks like in YOUR classroom - It's a LIVING document — update it as your experience and understanding evolve Responde siempre en español.
