Act as a research methodologist who designs surveys that collect actionable data AND analyzes results with statistical rigor — because a poorly designed survey produces confident-sounding nonsense. Design a survey and analyze results. INPUTS: - Research question: [WHAT YOU WANT TO LEARN] - Population: [WHO YOU'RE SURVEYING] - Sample size: [HOW MANY RESPONSES YOU EXPECT/NEED] - Survey type: [CUSTOMER SATISFACTION / MARKET RESEARCH / EMPLOYEE ENGAGEMENT / PRODUCT FEEDBACK] - Distribution method: [EMAIL / IN-APP / PANEL / SOCIAL MEDIA] - Analysis depth: [BASIC SUMMARY / CROSS-TABULATION / STATISTICAL TESTING / PREDICTIVE] DELIVERABLES: 1. **Survey Design:** | # | Question | Type | Scale | Why This Question | - 15-25 questions organized by section - Mix of question types: Likert, multiple choice, ranking, open-ended - Logical flow: easy → complex → demographic - Skip logic and branching rules 2. **Sample Size Calculation:** | Parameter | Value | Rationale | - Population size - Confidence level (95%) - Margin of error (±5%) - Expected response rate - Required sample size - Minimum per sub-group for meaningful comparison 3. **Analysis Plan:** | Question | Analysis Method | Deliverable | - Descriptive statistics for every question - Cross-tabulations for key segments - Correlation analysis between variables - Significance testing for group differences - Open-ended response coding and themes 4. **Results Template:** | Finding | Data | Significance | Confidence | Actionable Insight | - Key findings with supporting data - Visualizations: chart type per question type - Statistical significance of key differences - Quotes from open-ended responses (illustrative) 5. **Report Structure:** - Executive summary: top 3-5 findings - Methodology: sample, distribution, response rate, limitations - Detailed findings by section - Cross-segment analysis - Recommendations with evidence 6. **Bias Assessment:** | Bias Type | Risk Level | Mitigation | - Selection bias: who responded vs. who didn't - Response bias: social desirability, acquiescence - Order effects: question positioning - Non-response analysis CONSTRAINTS: - Every question must EARN its place — if you won't act on the answer, don't ask the question - Leading questions invalidate results — "How much did you love our awesome new feature?" is not research - Sample size matters for SEGMENTS — 100 total responses might be great overall but useless for sub-group analysis - Response rate IS data — a 5% response rate tells you something about the population you're ignoring - Statistical significance ≠ business significance — a statistically significant 2% difference may not matter for decisions Responde siempre en español.
