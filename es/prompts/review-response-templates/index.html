Act as a customer experience strategist who turns every review — positive, negative, or neutral — into an opportunity to strengthen your brand and win (or win back) customers. Create 10 review response templates for [BUSINESS TYPE]. INPUTS: - Business: [NAME AND TYPE] - Review platforms: [GOOGLE / YELP / TRUSTPILOT / G2 / APP STORE] - Common praise: [WHAT CUSTOMERS LOVE MOST] - Common complaints: [TOP 3 RECURRING ISSUES] - Response authority: [WHO RESPONDS — owner, manager, support team] - Fix capability: [CAN YOU ACTUALLY FIX THE ISSUES MENTIONED?] 10 RESPONSE TEMPLATES: | # | Review Type | Star Rating | Scenario | Template | **Positive Reviews (5 stars):** 1. **Raving Fan:** When they mention specific features and recommend to others. Template: Thank by name → acknowledge the specific thing they praised → share why that's important to your team → invite them back / suggest next step. 2. **Silent Satisfier:** When they leave 5 stars with minimal text. Template: Thank them → ask what they enjoyed most (encourages more detail) → extend an invitation. 3. **Loyal Returner:** When a repeat customer leaves a review. Template: Recognize their loyalty → recall their history if possible → offer something special for regulars. **Neutral Reviews (3-4 stars):** 4. **Mixed Experience:** When they liked some things but not others. Template: Thank for honesty → celebrate what went well → acknowledge what didn't → explain what you're doing to improve → invite them to try again. 5. **Expectation Gap:** When the product/service didn't match what they expected. Template: Empathize → clarify if expectation was reasonable → explain what to expect → offer to make it right. **Negative Reviews (1-2 stars):** 6. **Legitimate Complaint:** When they had a real, valid problem. Template: Apologize sincerely → don't make excuses → explain the fix → offer compensation → invite private follow-up → "We've already [SPECIFIC ACTION]." 7. **Angry and Emotional:** When they're upset and using strong language. Template: Stay calm → acknowledge their frustration → don't argue → take it offline → "I'd love to make this right — please reach out to [CONTACT]." 8. **Factually Incorrect:** When the review contains wrong information. Template: Politely correct the record → provide accurate info → don't accuse them of lying → offer clarification. 9. **Unfair/Unreasonable:** When the complaint isn't about something you can control. Template: Empathize with their frustration → gently explain the situation → offer what IS in your control → remain professional. 10. **Competitor Troll / Fake Review:** When you suspect the review isn't genuine. Template: Professional, factual response → "We don't have a record of this visit/purchase" → flag to platform via proper channels → NEVER accuse publicly. FOR EACH TEMPLATE: - Response time target: within 24 hours - Tone: professional but human (not corporate) - Personalization points: [NAME], [SPECIFIC DETAIL], [DATE] - Length: 50-100 words (concise, not defensive essays) - When to take it offline vs. resolve publicly CONSTRAINTS: - NEVER argue, be defensive, or be sarcastic in a review response - NEVER use copy-paste identical responses — personalize each one - NEVER offer free things in PUBLIC responses (encourages fake negative reviews) - Every negative review response must include a SPECIFIC action you're taking - Respond to ALL reviews — not just negative ones (positive reviews deserve thanks too) Responde siempre en español.
