Act as a direct-response copywriter who has written sales pages generating $1M+ in revenue. You write copy that makes readers feel understood before you ever ask them to buy. Write a high-converting sales page for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. INPUTS: - Product/Service: [WHAT IT IS AND WHAT IT DOES] - Price: $[AMOUNT] - Target buyer: [DESCRIBE YOUR IDEAL CUSTOMER — demographics, psychographics, pain level] - Main transformation: [WHAT LIFE LOOKS LIKE BEFORE vs. AFTER] - Top 3 objections: [WHAT STOPS PEOPLE FROM BUYING] - Social proof available: [TESTIMONIALS / CASE STUDIES / NUMBERS / LOGOS] - Urgency lever: [DEADLINE / LIMITED SPOTS / PRICE INCREASE / SEASONAL] SALES PAGE STRUCTURE: 1. **Pre-Headline:** Target the reader directly: "For [SPECIFIC PERSON] who [SPECIFIC SITUATION]" This filters out the wrong audience and makes the right audience lean in. 2. **Headline (3 options):** Formula: [DESIRED OUTCOME] + [TIMEFRAME] + [WITHOUT COMMON OBJECTION] - Option A: Benefit-driven - Option B: Curiosity-driven - Option C: Social-proof-driven Each headline must be under 15 words and make a SPECIFIC promise. 3. **Opening Story (200-300 words):** Tell a story that mirrors the reader's current frustration: - Start with a relatable moment ("You're staring at [SITUATION]...") - Make them feel SEEN ("And the worst part is...") - Create the "that's exactly me" moment Do NOT pitch the product here. Just connect. 4. **Problem Agitation (3 pain points):** For each: - The surface-level symptom (what they notice) - The deeper consequence (what it's costing them) - The emotional weight (how it makes them FEEL) Use specific language your audience uses. No generic pains. 5. **The Bridge (transition to solution):** "What if there was a way to [OUTCOME] without [BIGGEST OBJECTION]?" Introduce the solution WITHOUT naming it yet. Build anticipation. 6. **Product Reveal:** - Product name and one-line description - WHY this approach works (mechanism of action) - How it's DIFFERENT from everything else they've tried - 3-5 bullet points on what's included 7. **Features → Benefits Stack:** | Feature | Benefit ("So that you...") | Emotional Payoff | Write 5-7 features. Every feature must answer: "So what? Why should I care?" BAD: "50 video lessons" → GOOD: "50 video lessons so that you can master [X] in 30 minutes a day without overwhelm" 8. **Social Proof Section:** - 3 testimonials (structured: situation → action → result → recommendation) - Specific numbers: "Grew from $X to $Y in Z months" - Trust logos or media mentions (if available) - User count or results counter 9. **Bonuses Stack:** 3 bonuses with: | Bonus | Value | Why It's Included | - Each bonus should solve a secondary objection - Total bonus value should exceed the product price - "Included FREE when you join today" 10. **Price Anchoring:** - Stack the total value: "Total value: $[HIGH NUMBER]" - Reveal the actual price: "Today's investment: $[PRICE]" - Break it down: "That's just $[DAILY AMOUNT] per day" - Compare to alternatives: "Less than [ONE THING THEY ALREADY SPEND ON]" 11. **Risk Reversal (Guarantee):** - Specific guarantee: 30-day, 60-day, or results-based - Name the guarantee (e.g., "The Zero-Risk Promise") - Make it stronger than expected (return + keep the bonuses) - Address the fear directly: "You risk nothing. I risk everything." 12. **Urgency/Scarcity:** Must be REAL (not manufactured): - Deadline: "Price increases on [DATE]" - Limited: "Only [X] spots available (currently [Y] remaining)" - Bonus expiry: "Bonuses removed after [DATE]" 13. **Final CTA:** - Button text: action-oriented, NOT "Submit" or "Buy Now" - Examples: "Start My Transformation" / "Get Instant Access" / "Join 2,000+ Members" - Repeat the guarantee below the button - P.S. line: restate the biggest benefit + urgency CONSTRAINTS: - Do NOT use hype language: "amazing", "revolutionary", "game-changing" — show, don't tell - Do NOT make claims you can't back up — every number must be real or clearly hypothetical - Do NOT write wall-of-text paragraphs — max 3 sentences per paragraph for scanability - Do NOT use "Dear Friend" or outdated sales letter openings - Write at a 6th-8th grade reading level (Hemingway App score: Good) - Every section must be scannable with bold, bullets, and white space Responde siempre en español.
