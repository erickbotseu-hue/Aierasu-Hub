Utilidad de alto valor para tareas de Creatividad.

🚀 Consejo Pro

Consejo Pro: Optimiza tus resultados iterando el prompt.

Act as a showrunner who creates series bibles that make executives say "yes" and writers' rooms say "I know exactly what this show is" — because a great series bible is both a sales tool and a creative blueprint. Create a series bible/show pitch for a [GENRE] TV series. INPUTS: - Genre: [DRAMA / COMEDY / THRILLER / SCI-FI / HORROR / DRAMEDY] - Logline: [THE SHOW IN ONE SENTENCE — or "help me develop one"] - Tone: [REFERENCE SHOWS — "the tone of X meets the style of Y"] - Format: [HALF-HOUR / HOUR / LIMITED SERIES] - Seasons planned: [1 / 3 / 5+] - Platform fit: [HBO / NETFLIX / NETWORK / STREAMING / CABLE] SERIES BIBLE: 1. **Logline:** One sentence that sells the show 2. **Premise (1 page):** - What's the show ABOUT? (not just "what happens" — what does it EXPLORE?) - The world and why it's compelling - The engine: what generates NEW stories week after week? 3. **Main Characters (3-6):** | Character | Role | Actor Type | Want | Need | Arc | For each character: - Who they are in the pilot - Who they'll become by season finale - Their relationships with other characters - Why an ACTOR would want to play this role 4. **Pilot Episode:** | Act | What Happens | Character Moments | Hooks | - Beat-by-beat outline of the first episode - How it establishes the world, characters, and engine - The final scene: what question does the audience leave with? 5. **Season 1 Arc:** | Episode | Title | A-Story | B-Story | Character Focus | - Brief description of each episode - How the season builds to its climax - The season finale: what's resolved and what's left open 6. **Future Seasons (brief):** | Season | Focus | Theme | Big Event | - Where the show goes from here - How the world/characters expand - The long-game questions the show explores over multiple seasons 7. **Comparable Shows:** [X] meets [Y] with the specificity of [Z] 8. **Why Now:** Why this show matters RIGHT NOW — cultural relevance and timeliness CONSTRAINTS: - The "engine" is paramount — what generates REPEATABLE stories? (a new case each week, a new challenge, a deepening mystery) - Characters must be CASTABLE — write characters actors will fight over - The pilot must work as a MOVIE — it's a standalone story that opens a bigger world - Be honest about difficulty — if it's expensive (VFX, locations), address it - The series bible should make someone who reads it ANGRY they can't watch the show yet Responde siempre en español.
